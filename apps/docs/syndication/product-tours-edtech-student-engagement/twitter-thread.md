**2/** The core problem: students, instructors, and admins share one interface with completely different goals.

A generic 10-step tour teaches nobody. Route by role at first login — one question is enough to customize the entire onboarding path.

**3/** Front-loaded tooltip tours get dismissed. Contextual hints (triggered when a user reaches a feature) see 50% higher activation.

Don't show everything on day one. Show the right thing at the right time.

**4/** Starting April 24, 2026, all public educational institutions must comply with WCAG 2.1 AA.

That includes product tours. Keyboard navigation, screen readers, reduced motion — all required by law, not just best practice.

**5/** EdTech usage is cyclical. Semester starts, breaks, returns.

Firing the same onboarding at a returning student? That trains them to close every overlay. Track login history. New students get full onboarding. Returning students get "what's new" only.
