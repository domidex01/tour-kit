## Title: EdTech onboarding completion rates average 15.9% – patterns that improve student engagement

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/product-tours-edtech-student-engagement

## Comment to post immediately after:

I've been researching onboarding data for education platforms. Userpilot benchmarked 188 companies and the EdTech numbers stood out — 15.9% onboarding checklist completion, trailing the 19.2% cross-industry average and far behind FinTech at 24.5%.

The fundamental challenge is multi-persona: students, instructors, and admins share the same interface with completely different goals. Most platforms handle this with a generic tooltip tour that tries to show everything. The data says that doesn't work.

The patterns that do: role-based routing (one question at login, different tour paths), contextual hints triggered by behavior instead of front-loaded walkthroughs, and semester-aware fatigue prevention (returning students shouldn't see the same onboarding again).

One timely angle: the April 24, 2026 WCAG 2.1 AA deadline under ADA Title II means every public educational institution's web content — including product tours and modal overlays — must be keyboard navigable, screen reader compatible, and respect prefers-reduced-motion. It's a legal requirement, not a nice-to-have.

Disclosure: I built Tour Kit (an open-source React product tour library), so I'm biased. But the benchmark data and patterns apply regardless of which library you use.
