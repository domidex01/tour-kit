Your support queue tells you where onboarding failed.

Most SaaS support tickets are "how do I" questions with deterministic answers. The answer exists in docs. The user couldn't find it while using the product.

Product tours fix this by putting guidance on the same screen as the action. The economics are hard to ignore: Gartner puts self-service at $0.10/interaction vs $8.01 for agent-assisted tickets.

Growth Mentor cut support tickets by 83% after implementing self-service onboarding. Across B2B SaaS, the typical range is 30-50% reduction for feature discovery and workflow questions.

Five patterns that work: contextual triggers (start when users are stuck, not when they land on a page), first-action tours (one workflow, not a feature dump), persistent hints, role-based branching, and checklists with dependency ordering.

The ROI math for engineering teams: 1,000 tickets/month, 40% deflectable = ~$37,500/year in direct support cost savings. Most orgs see payback within 6 months.
