**1/** The average SaaS activation rate is 36%.

That means nearly two-thirds of your signups leave before they experience any product value.

Here's what user activation actually means and why most teams measure it wrong:

**2/** User activation = the moment a user completes a specific action that predicts long-term retention.

Not "logged in." Not "watched tutorial."

Slack's was 2,000 team messages (93% retention for teams hitting it). Trello's was "4 items in 28 days."

**3/** ProductLed's three-part test for a good activation event:

- Engagement-based (active product use)
- Time-bound (within a defined window)
- Process-signaling (sustainable behavior forming)

If your activation rate is 80%+ but churn is high — wrong event.

**4/** Why it matters for revenue:

25% improvement in activation = ~34% MRR growth over 12 months.

75% of users churn in the first week.

Activation is the most time-sensitive metric you have.

**5/** Common mistake: confusing activation with engagement.

Engagement = ongoing (DAU/MAU, session length)
Activation = binary milestone (crossed the value threshold or didn't)

You can have high engagement from a small base and still bleed signups. Fix activation first.
