Two-thirds of SaaS signups never experience any product value.

The average activation rate across SaaS is just 36%. That's nearly two out of three users leaving before they understand why your product exists.

User activation is the moment a user does something specific that predicts they'll come back. Slack's activation event was 2,000 team messages. Trello's was creating 4 items within 28 days. None of these are "completed the tutorial" — they're real product usage.

The revenue math is compelling: a 25% improvement in activation drives roughly 34% MRR growth over 12 months. And with 75% of users churning in the first week, you don't have much time to get them there.

Most teams either pick the wrong activation event (easy to measure but doesn't predict retention) or don't define one at all.
