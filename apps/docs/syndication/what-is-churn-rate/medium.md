# What Is Churn Rate? The Metric That Tells You Whether Onboarding Works

### 70% of SaaS churn happens in the first 90 days. Here's what to do about it.

*Originally published at [usertourkit.com](https://usertourkit.com/blog/what-is-churn-rate)*

Seventy percent of SaaS churn happens in the first 90 days. Not month six. Not after a competitor steals the deal. The first three months. That makes churn rate less of a retention metric and more of an onboarding diagnostic.

## What churn rate actually means

Churn rate is the percentage of customers who cancel, downgrade, or stop using a product during a given period. It's typically measured monthly for SaaS businesses. As of April 2026, median B2B SaaS monthly churn sits at 3.5%, with SMBs running 3-7% and enterprise accounts at 0.5-1%.

Two flavors matter. **Customer churn** counts heads. Ten customers left out of 500? That's 2%. Simple, but it treats a $50/month account the same as a $50,000/month one. **Revenue churn** (MRR churn) counts dollars, giving you the financial picture that customer churn hides.

## The formulas

The basic calculation:

> Churn Rate = (Customers Lost in Period / Customers at Start of Period) x 100

Start April with 900 customers and lose 30? That's 3.33% monthly churn.

One thing trips people up: monthly-to-annual conversion isn't multiplication. 5% monthly churn doesn't equal 60% annual. It compounds to roughly 46%. At 2% monthly, that drops to about 21%. Small monthly improvements compound into large annual gains.

## Where you stand: 2026 benchmarks

From a 2026 study of 500+ SaaS companies:

- **Enterprise (>$100K ACV):** 0.5-1% monthly, 6-10% annual
- **Mid-market ($15K-$100K ACV):** 1-3% monthly, 11-22% annual
- **SMB (<$15K ACV):** 3-7% monthly, 31-58% annual
- **Early-stage (<$1M ARR):** 5-7% monthly, 46-58% annual

Annual churn below 5% is gold standard for established B2B SaaS. If you're early-stage and above 7% monthly, onboarding is the first place to look.

## Why onboarding is the fix

Most churn isn't a retention problem. It's an activation problem wearing a retention mask.

75% of users abandon a product within the first week when onboarding fails to show clear value. Users who complete full onboarding retain at 82%. Partial onboarding? Just 19%. A 4x gap driven by whether someone understood the product before quitting.

Personalized onboarding lifts 90-day retention by 41%. Companies where time-to-first-value sits under seven days see 50% lower churn rates.

Google's developer blog documented this concretely: the Fabulous app ran A/B tests on their onboarding using Firebase Remote Config, lifting completion from 42% to 64% and boosting day-one retention by 27%.

## Product tours as a churn prevention tool

Product tours address churn at the exact moment it's decided: the first session. The pattern that works: guide users to one high-value action rather than narrating the entire UI. Directing users to a single "aha moment" cut Day 1 churn by 20% in one case study, and each day shaved off time-to-value increased activation rate by 3-5%.
