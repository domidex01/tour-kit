70% of SaaS churn happens in the first 90 days.

That surprised me. Most teams treat churn as a retention problem — building win-back campaigns, loyalty programs, exit surveys. But the data says it's an activation problem.

Users who complete full onboarding retain at 82%. Those who only partially onboard? 19%. A 4x gap.

The 2026 benchmarks from 500+ SaaS companies show how much segment matters too:
- Enterprise: 0.5-1% monthly churn
- Mid-market: 1-3%
- SMB: 3-7%
- Early-stage: 5-7%

One finding I keep coming back to: personalized onboarding lifts 90-day retention by 41%. And companies with time-to-first-value under 7 days see 50% lower churn.

The first session matters more than the last one.
