## Thread (6 tweets)

**1/** 70% of SaaS churn happens in the first 90 days.

Not month six. Not after a competitor steals the deal. The first three months.

That makes churn rate less of a retention metric and more of an onboarding diagnostic.

**2/** The 2026 benchmarks (500+ companies):

Enterprise (>$100K ACV): 0.5-1% monthly
Mid-market: 1-3% monthly
SMB: 3-7% monthly
Early-stage: 5-7% monthly

Annual churn below 5% = gold standard.

**3/** Math trap: 5% monthly churn ≠ 60% annual.

It compounds: 1 - (1 - 0.05)^12 = ~46%

At 2% monthly → ~21% annual.

Small monthly improvements compound into large annual gains.

**4/** The onboarding gap is wild:

Full onboarding completers → 82% retention
Partial onboarding → 19% retention

That's a 4x gap driven by whether users understood the product before quitting.

**5/** What works for reducing churn:

- Personalized onboarding → +41% 90-day retention
- Time-to-value under 7 days → 50% lower churn
- Guiding to one "aha moment" → -20% Day 1 churn

Cap onboarding at 5 steps (cognitive load ceiling).
