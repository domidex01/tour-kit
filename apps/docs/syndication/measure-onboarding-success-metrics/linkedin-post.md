59% of SaaS buyers regret a recent software purchase. Most of that regret forms in the first 30 days.

I analyzed benchmark data from 15 million product tour interactions and 547 SaaS companies to find the onboarding metrics that actually predict retention.

Three findings that changed how I think about onboarding:

1. Four-step tours outperform three-step tours (74% vs 72% completion). Shorter isn't always better — users need enough value delivered to justify the interruption.

2. Sales-led growth companies achieve faster time-to-value (1d 11h) than product-led growth companies (1d 12h). PLG doesn't automatically mean faster activation.

3. Self-serve tours complete at 123% higher rates than auto-triggered ones. User agency matters more than timing optimization.

If you're only tracking three onboarding metrics, make them: tour completion rate, time to value, and 30-day retention. Those three form a funnel that reveals exactly where users drop off.
