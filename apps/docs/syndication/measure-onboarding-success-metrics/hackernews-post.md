## Title: Onboarding Metrics That Matter: Benchmarks from 15M Tour Interactions and 547 SaaS Companies

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/measure-onboarding-success-metrics

## Comment to post immediately after:

I pulled together benchmark data from two large datasets — Chameleon's analysis of 15 million product tour interactions and Userpilot's study of 547 SaaS companies — to figure out which onboarding metrics actually predict retention.

A few findings I found genuinely surprising:

1. 4-step tours outperform 3-step tours in completion rate (74% vs 72%). The assumption that shorter is always better doesn't hold — tours need to deliver enough value to justify the interruption.

2. Sales-led growth companies achieve marginally faster time-to-value (1d 11h) than product-led growth companies (1d 12h). This contradicts the common narrative that PLG automatically means faster activation.

3. Self-serve tours (user-initiated via hotspot or button click) complete at 123% higher rates than auto-triggered tours. User agency appears to be a stronger predictor of engagement than timing optimization.

The article covers 10 metrics total with formulas, industry-segmented benchmarks (HR platforms take 3d 18h to reach value vs 1d 4h for CRM), and React code for instrumenting each one. I built Tour Kit (the library used in the code examples), so adjust for that bias accordingly.

Interested in what metrics other HN folks track during onboarding, and whether these benchmarks match your experience.
