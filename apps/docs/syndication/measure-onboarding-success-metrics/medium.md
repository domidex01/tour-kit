# How to Measure Onboarding Success: 10 Metrics That Matter

## Most SaaS teams track signups and churn. The gap between those two numbers is where onboarding lives.

*Originally published at [usertourkit.com](https://usertourkit.com/blog/measure-onboarding-success-metrics)*

As of April 2026, 59% of SaaS buyers regret at least one software purchase made in the past 18 months (Gartner 2025). Most of that regret crystallizes in the first 30 days, during onboarding.

This guide covers the 10 metrics that actually predict whether your onboarding works. Each one includes the formula and benchmark data from real studies.

## Why onboarding metrics matter

Companies with structured onboarding see 62% faster time-to-productivity. Litmus saw a 2,100% increase in feature adoption after instrumenting their onboarding flow. Yotpo improved retention by 50%. Take.net boosted activation rate by 124%.

These aren't outliers. They're what happens when teams stop guessing.

## The 10 metrics at a glance

**1. Tour completion rate** — Completions / Tour starts x 100. Average: 61% (Chameleon, 15M interactions). 4-step tours peak at 74%.

**2. Time to value** — Date of first value action minus signup date. Average: 1d 12h 23m across 547 SaaS companies (Userpilot).

**3. Activation rate** — Activated users / Onboarding cohort x 100. No universal benchmark — define your "aha moment" first.

**4. Feature adoption rate** — Feature users / Eligible users x 100. Key: only count users who can access the feature.

**5. Funnel drop-off** — Users lost at step / Users entering step x 100. Industry average: 38% drop at stage 1 to 2.

**6. 30-day retention** — Users at day 30 / Initial cohort x 100. Good B2B: 40-60%.

**7. Free trial conversion** — Converters / Trial users x 100. Industry average: 17%.

**8. Customer effort score** — Average score on 1-7 scale. Below 3 is excellent.

**9. Onboarding NPS** — % Promoters minus % Detractors. B2B SaaS median: 30-40.

**10. Support ticket volume** — Tickets in first 14 days / New users. No benchmark; track the trend.

## Start with three

You don't need all 10 metrics on day one:

1. Tour completion rate (are users finishing what you built?)
2. Time to value (how fast do they get there?)
3. 30-day retention (do they stick around?)

These three form a funnel: completion feeds activation, activation feeds retention.

## The surprising findings

Self-serve tours (user-initiated) complete at 123% higher rates than auto-triggered tours. And sales-led growth companies (1d 11h) achieved slightly faster time to value than product-led growth companies (1d 12h) — challenging conventional wisdom.
