**1/** 59% of SaaS buyers regret a recent software purchase. Most of that regret forms in the first 30 days — during onboarding.

I pulled benchmark data from 15M product tour interactions to find the 10 metrics that actually predict retention.

**2/** Tour completion rate: Average is 61%. But step count matters more than you'd think.

3-step tours: 72%
4-step tours: 74%
7+ step tours: 16%

4 steps beats 3. Too short = not enough value delivered.

(Source: Chameleon, 15M interactions)

**3/** Time to value across 547 SaaS companies:

CRM: 1d 4h
Healthcare: 1d 7h
Fintech: 1d 17h
HR: 3d 18h

And here's the kicker: sales-led companies (1d 11h) were faster than product-led (1d 12h).

PLG doesn't automatically mean faster activation.

**4/** The most underused metric: Customer Effort Score (CES).

Fire a 1-7 scale survey immediately after onboarding. Response rate drops 60% if you wait 24 hours.

Below 3 = low friction. Above 5 = your onboarding is fighting the user.

**5/** Start with just 3 metrics:

1. Tour completion rate (are they finishing?)
2. Time to value (how fast?)
3. 30-day retention (do they stick?)

These form a funnel. Fix where it leaks before adding more complexity.
