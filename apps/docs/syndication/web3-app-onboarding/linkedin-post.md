55% of users abandon during wallet setup. 65% leave during their first transaction. Sub-1% 30-day retention is common across web3 projects.

The wallet infrastructure has improved — embedded wallets, account abstraction, social login flows. But there's a gap nobody's filling: what happens AFTER the user connects their wallet?

Most dApp teams invest heavily in wallet connection and then drop users into complex dashboards with zero contextual help.

Product tours fill that gap, but standard approaches break in web3:
- Blockchain transactions are async (15s to minutes)
- Wallet interactions happen in external popups outside your DOM
- Different wallet types need completely different instructions
- Tour copy carries legal/compliance weight it doesn't carry in SaaS

I wrote up the five patterns that work: delayed wallet connection, async-safe tour steps, wallet-type branching, jargon-busting hints, and network context guards. With code examples.
