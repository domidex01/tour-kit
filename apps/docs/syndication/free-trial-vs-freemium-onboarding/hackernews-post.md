## Title: Free trial vs freemium onboarding: different psychology, different engineering

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/free-trial-vs-freemium-onboarding

## Comment to post immediately after:

I wrote this after noticing that most onboarding guides treat trial and freemium users identically. They're not — the user psychology is fundamentally different, and the engineering needs to match.

The core finding: trial onboarding is urgency-driven (hit activation in 30-40% of trial duration), while freemium onboarding is patience-based (gate value amplifiers, not basic utility). 65% of PLG SaaS now runs hybrid models combining both, which creates real engineering complexity around dual entitlement states and conditional onboarding paths.

The accessibility angle was the most surprising gap. I searched for WCAG-compliant onboarding design guidance across major publications — every result was about accessibility tools offering free trials, not about making onboarding flows accessible. Countdown timers, focus management, and reduced-motion support in onboarding are essentially undocumented.

Article includes React code examples for all three patterns (trial, freemium, hybrid) and a 12-row comparison table with conversion benchmarks.

I build Tour Kit (headless product tour library for React), so there's a bias disclaimer in the article. The data and patterns are framework-agnostic though.
