Most onboarding guides assume horizontal SaaS — drop in a tooltip library, show 5 steps, done.

Vertical SaaS doesn't work that way.

I spent a few weeks mapping onboarding patterns for healthcare, fintech, and edtech applications. The compliance and architectural differences are bigger than I expected:

→ In healthcare, every tool in your onboarding stack needs a HIPAA Business Associate Agreement. Third-party scripts become data processors.

→ In fintech, product tours rendering inside PCI-scoped iframes accidentally expand your PCI assessment scope. Shine achieved 80% onboarding conversion while the industry benchmark sits at 21%.

→ In edtech, a teacher and a student use entirely different features — role-based branching isn't a nice-to-have. WCAG became mandatory for EU SaaS in June 2025.

The market tells the story: vertical SaaS hit $143.45 billion in 2026, growing 2-3x faster than horizontal tools. But most onboarding tooling hasn't caught up with the compliance and domain requirements these products face.

Users who complete onboarding are 80% more likely to become long-term customers. In vertical SaaS where annual contracts run five or six figures, that retention gap is revenue.
