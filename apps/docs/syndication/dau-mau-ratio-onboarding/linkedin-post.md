Only 12% of SaaS users rate their onboarding as "effective."

The average SaaS DAU/MAU ratio is 13%. These aren't separate problems.

I researched the connection between product onboarding quality and stickiness metrics. Some of the data from Chameleon's analysis of 15 million product tour interactions:

- Interactive tours increase feature adoption by 42%
- Structured onboarding lifts retention by 50%
- Users who activate within 3 days are 90% more likely to retain long-term

The counterintuitive finding: tour completion rate is a vanity metric. What actually predicts stickiness is whether the tour drove the user to their activation behavior, not whether they clicked through every step.

One nuance most benchmark articles miss: B2B products used Monday through Friday have a theoretical maximum DAU/MAU of ~71%. A raw 15% ratio is actually ~21% effective engagement when adjusted. Comparing weekday-only tools to 7-day consumer apps on raw numbers is misleading.
