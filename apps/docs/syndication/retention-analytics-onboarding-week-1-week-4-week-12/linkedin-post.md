Your onboarding tour has a 72% completion rate. But how many of those users still exist in your product three months later?

I compiled retention benchmarks from Mixpanel, Amplitude, and Appcues to answer this question at three checkpoints: week 1, week 4, and week 12.

The headline finding: the median SaaS product retains just 15% of users at week 12. Products with structured onboarding see roughly 2x that number at every checkpoint. But it's correlation, not causation. The more useful metric is splitting your users into tour completers vs. skippers and measuring the retention delta.

Week 4 turned out to be the most interesting inflection point. Amplitude's data shows it's the single strongest predictor of 12-month LTV. And developer tools specifically face a steeper week 4 drop than general B2B SaaS, suggesting an "integration cliff" that standard onboarding advice doesn't address.
