## Title: Retention analytics for onboarding: week 1 vs week 4 vs week 12

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/retention-analytics-onboarding-week-1-week-4-week-12

## Comment to post immediately after:

I compiled retention benchmark data from Mixpanel's 2025 Product Benchmarks, Amplitude's 2024 report, and Appcues' cohort studies to build a reference for what "good" onboarding retention looks like at three checkpoints.

The key finding that surprised me: the median SaaS product only retains 15% of users at week 12, but products with structured onboarding see ~22%. That 7 percentage point gap compounds significantly over a year. Developer tools specifically show a steeper week 4 drop than general B2B SaaS, suggesting an "integration cliff" that standard onboarding advice doesn't address.

The most actionable piece: splitting your users into completers vs. skippers and comparing their retention curves. The delta tells you whether your onboarding is actually moving the needle or whether you're just seeing self-selection bias.

Full disclosure: I build Tour Kit, a headless product tour library for React, so the code examples use Tour Kit's API. The benchmarks and methodology are tool-agnostic though.
