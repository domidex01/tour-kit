# Product Tour Completion Rate: What the Benchmarks Actually Say

## And why adding a fifth step to your tour cuts completion in half

*Originally published at [usertourkit.com](https://usertourkit.com/blog/product-tour-completion-rate-benchmarks)*

Most product teams track whether users finish their onboarding tours. Few know what "good" looks like. The largest public dataset on tour completion — 15 million interactions analyzed by Chameleon — puts the average at 61%. But that number hides a brutal step-count cliff.

Tours with 3–4 steps hit 72–74% completion. Add a fifth step and that drops to 34%. Not 64%. Not 54%. Thirty-four percent.

## The step-count cliff

Chameleon analyzed 15 million product tour interactions and published the only large-scale public dataset on completion rates. The pattern is clear:

- **2–3 steps:** 72% completion
- **4 steps:** 74% completion (the peak)
- **5 steps:** 34% completion
- **7+ steps:** 16% completion

That jump from 4 to 5 steps is the most important number in onboarding analytics. Completion doesn't degrade linearly. It falls off a cliff.

## How the tour starts matters as much as how long it runs

Self-serve tours — where users choose to start from a help menu or "show me how" button — outperform auto-triggered tours by more than double. User-initiated tours hit about 67% completion. Time-delayed auto-play tours hit 31%.

The takeaway is simple: when users choose to learn, they finish. When you interrupt them, they dismiss.

## The checklist gap nobody talks about

Userpilot's 2024 Product Metrics Report found that onboarding checklist completion averages just 19.2%. Compare that to tour completion at 61%. A checklist asks for repeated effort across sessions. A tour asks for 30 seconds of attention right now.

But here's the interesting part: checklist-triggered tours are 21% more likely to be completed than average. The checklist provides the motivation, and the tour provides the how.

## Five patterns that actually move the number

1. **Keep tours under 5 steps.** The data is unambiguous.
2. **Let users initiate tours.** Self-serve tours complete 123% more often.
3. **Add progress indicators.** +12% completion, -20% dismissal.
4. **Use contextual triggers over time delays.** On-page action triggers hit 69.56% completion vs. 31% for auto-play.
5. **Connect tours to checklists.** 60% of users who complete a checklist-triggered tour go on to finish multiple tasks.

## The caveats

The Chameleon dataset is from 2019 and skewed toward SaaS companies. As practitioners on the Intercom Community noted: "There is no single answer, not even a range of % you should expect."

And the industry is shifting away from completion rate as the primary metric. About 70% of users skip traditional linear tours entirely, which means completion rate captures only the 30% who opted in.

Use the benchmarks as a diagnostic starting point. If you're below 50%, fix the tour. If you're above 70%, start measuring whether completers actually adopt the feature.
