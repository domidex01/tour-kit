I tested 8 alternatives to Chameleon for product tours and ran accessibility audits on each one.

The pricing range shocked me. UserGuiding starts at $69/month while Chameleon averages $30,000/year at scale (Vendr data). That's a 36x difference for tools in the same category.

But the bigger finding was accessibility. I ran axe-core against every SaaS tour overlay on the list. All of them failed with missing ARIA attributes, broken focus management, and no keyboard navigation. None document WCAG compliance.

For engineering leaders evaluating these tools: the accessibility gap matters. If your product ships tours that screen reader users can't navigate, that's a liability. And none of the SaaS vendors address it.

The full comparison covers Tour Kit (mine, so bias disclosed), Appcues, Userpilot, Pendo, UserGuiding, Product Fruits, Shepherd.js, and React Joyride with pricing, bundle sizes, and React 19 support data.
