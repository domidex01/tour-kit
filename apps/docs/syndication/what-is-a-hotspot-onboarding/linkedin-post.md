Less than 20% of page copy gets read (UX Myths). So why are we still building text-heavy onboarding flows?

Hotspots solve this. They're the opt-in alternative to modals: a small pulsing dot that reveals guidance only when the user clicks. Zero interruption until they choose to engage.

The part most teams miss: WCAG 2.1 SC 1.4.13 requires three things for any content on hover or focus — dismissible via Escape, hoverable without disappearing, and no timeout-based hiding. Most implementations skip all three.

And here's the twist: hotspots are actually more accessible on mobile than tooltips, because they use tap instead of hover (which doesn't exist on touch devices).
