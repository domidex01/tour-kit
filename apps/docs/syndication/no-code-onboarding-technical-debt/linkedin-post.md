No-code onboarding tools create a category of technical debt that nobody talks about.

Your team sets up Appcues or Pendo. Looks great. Six months later: broken tooltips after a UI update, CSS overrides in a dashboard nobody can find, and the PM who configured everything just left the company.

I researched the long-term costs and found five patterns that keep repeating: CSS maintenance outside version control, vendor lock-in on tour logic, knowledge silos, analytics that still need engineering work, and accessibility gaps no dashboard can fix.

The industry frames this as "build from scratch ($55K/yr) vs buy SaaS ($12K-$50K/yr)." But that's a false binary. Code-first libraries break the model — one sprint of engineering time, no per-MAU subscription, full control over CSS and accessibility.

Gartner says 50% of apps still carry avoidable technical debt. Enterprises are creating "low-code governance" roles just to manage the mess. That's not a feature — that's a cost center.

If your engineering team has React developers and your product will exist in two years, it's worth asking: where does the onboarding CSS live, who maintains the tours, and what happens when you outgrow the tool?
