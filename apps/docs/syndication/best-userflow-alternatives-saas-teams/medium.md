# 7 Userflow Alternatives Your SaaS Team Should Know About in 2026

## Pricing, performance, and React compatibility compared side by side

*Originally published at [usertourkit.com](https://usertourkit.com/blog/best-userflow-alternatives-saas-teams)*

Userflow starts at $240/month for 3,000 MAUs and climbs past $1,000/month once your app hits 50,000 users. That MAU-based pricing model charges for every visitor, whether they see a product tour or not.

We installed and evaluated seven alternatives. Full disclosure: Tour Kit is our project. We've tried to be fair, but you should know that going in.

## The comparison at a glance

**Tour Kit** (Open source, free / $99 Pro) — Best for React teams wanting code ownership. Under 8KB gzipped. WCAG 2.1 AA compliant. No MAU limits.

**Userpilot** ($299/mo, 2K MAUs) — Best for PM-led teams. Bundles product analytics with onboarding.

**Appcues** ($249/mo, 1K MAUs) — Only platform with native iOS and Android SDKs.

**Chameleon** ($279/mo, 2K MTUs) — Deepest CSS customization. 60+ integrations.

**UserGuiding** ($174/mo) — Most affordable SaaS option. Covers basics well.

**Shepherd.js** (Free, AGPL) — Framework-agnostic. Supports React, Vue, Angular, Ember. AGPL license is the catch.

**React Joyride** (Free, MIT) — Largest community (603K+ weekly downloads). Not compatible with React 19.

## How to choose

Choose a SaaS platform if your product team creates tours without developers. Choose an open-source library if your engineering team owns onboarding and you want zero MAU costs.

One thing missing from every SaaS tool: documented accessibility compliance. None publish WCAG conformance information.
