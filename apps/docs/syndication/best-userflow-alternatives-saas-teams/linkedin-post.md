Product tour tools are quietly expensive at scale.

Userflow starts at $240/month for 3,000 MAUs. That number crosses $1,000/month once your app reaches 50,000 users. And you're paying for every visitor, whether they see a tour or not.

I spent a few weeks testing 7 alternatives (4 SaaS platforms, 3 open-source libraries) on pricing, React 19 support, accessibility, and analytics flexibility. The comparison covers Userpilot, Appcues, Chameleon, UserGuiding, Tour Kit, Shepherd.js, and React Joyride.

Two findings that surprised me:

1. None of the SaaS platforms publish WCAG accessibility documentation. For teams building products that serve government, healthcare, or enterprise customers, that's a real gap.

2. React Joyride still dominates downloads (603K/week) but doesn't support React 19. The React tour ecosystem hasn't caught up to the migration.

Full comparison with pricing tables and a decision framework: https://usertourkit.com/blog/best-userflow-alternatives-saas-teams
