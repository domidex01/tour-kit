## Title: The median SaaS product has 9.3% DAU/MAU stickiness – benchmarks by industry

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/what-is-product-stickiness

## Comment to post immediately after:

I put together a breakdown of product stickiness benchmarks because I kept seeing the same misleading numbers circulated without context. The median SaaS stickiness rate is 9.3% (Userpilot data), but that number is near-meaningless without knowing the category.

The biggest surprise was the B2B workday adjustment. Raw calendar-day DAU/MAU for B2B SaaS is 10-20%, but once you adjust for weekends/holidays the real benchmark jumps to ~40%. Most articles don't surface this distinction, which makes B2B founders compare themselves unfairly against consumer apps.

David Sacks makes a good point that DAU/MAU is inherently lagging due to the MAU denominator — not great for measuring the impact of specific product changes. The Lness distribution (L4+/7, L19+/28) gives more granular signal for shorter feedback loops.

Sources are primarily Geckoboard, Userpilot, Statsig, Gainsight, and Amplitude. Happy to discuss methodology or if anyone has seen different numbers in specific verticals.
