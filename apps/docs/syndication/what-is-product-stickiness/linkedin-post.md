The median SaaS product has a 9.3% stickiness rate.

That sounds terrible until you realize most benchmarks are misleading.

I dug into the data from Geckoboard, Userpilot, Statsig, and Gainsight. The biggest finding for B2B teams: raw calendar-day DAU/MAU punishes products that aren't used on weekends. A PM tool with perfect weekday engagement still maxes out at 71%.

Once you adjust for workdays, the real B2B benchmark jumps from 13% to roughly 40%. Most articles don't make this distinction.

Three patterns that actually move the number:
- Users who activate within 3 days are 90% more likely to keep using the product
- Personalized onboarding creates early habit loops
- Feedback collected after milestones (not mid-task) catches friction before churn
