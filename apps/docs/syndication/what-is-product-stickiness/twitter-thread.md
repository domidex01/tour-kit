**1/** The median SaaS product has a 9.3% stickiness rate (DAU/MAU).

But that number is almost useless without context. Here's what the benchmarks actually look like by industry:

**2/** Social/messaging: 50-80%
Productivity: 40-60%
B2B SaaS (workday-adjusted): ~40%
Gaming: 30-40%
B2B SaaS (raw): 10-20%
E-commerce: ~9.8%

Comparing across categories = comparing apples to office chairs.

**3/** The biggest B2B trap: calendar-day DAU/MAU punishes products not used on weekends.

A PM tool with perfect weekday engagement maxes out at 71% (5/7 days).

Gainsight recommends workday-adjusting the denominator. Real B2B benchmark: ~40%, not 13%.

**4/** David Sacks flags another problem: DAU/MAU is inherently lagging because the MAU denominator smooths out short-term changes.

Shipped a feature yesterday? DAU/MAU won't tell you if it worked for weeks.

Better alternative for fast feedback: the Lness distribution (L4+/7, L19+/28).

**5/** Three things that actually move stickiness:

1. Onboarding speed — users activating within 3 days are 90% more likely to stick
2. Habit loops — personalized onboarding adapts to user roles
3. Feedback timing — survey after milestones, not mid-task
