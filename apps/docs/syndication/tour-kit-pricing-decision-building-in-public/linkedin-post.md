I need 54 sales a month to sustain my open source project. Here's the math.

Tour Kit is a React product tour library. 10 packages, MIT core, TypeScript-first. The free tier covers everything a solo developer needs. The Pro tier ($99 one-time) adds organizational features like surveys, checklists, and adoption tracking.

Every pricing guide says "charge monthly." But charging a subscription for npm packages that already ship in your bundle felt wrong. SlashData data backs this up: 44% of developers prefer one-time purchases for tools that ship as packages.

$99 lands in what I call the "I'll expense this" zone — above impulse-buy territory ($20-$49), below the manager-approval threshold ($100+). It's where react-admin and Tailwind UI live, and react-admin does roughly 1M EUR/year from open-core.

The hardest part wasn't the price. It was drawing the free/paid boundary. The n8n pricing controversy showed what happens when you get that wrong. Tour Kit's split maps to who benefits: individual devs get everything free, product teams pay for organizational tooling.

After Polar.sh fees (4% + $0.40), I net $95.64 per sale. At $85/month in costs and a $5,000/month target, sustainability = 54 licenses/month.

That's the whole thing. No venture math. No "if we hit 100K users" projections.
