75% of users leave your app in week one. Only 12% rate their onboarding as "effective."

I published a developer-focused handbook on user onboarding that covers the patterns, metrics, and implementation decisions that actually move activation rates.

Key findings from the research:

A 25% improvement in activation translates to 34% higher revenue. Interactive onboarding flows outperform static walkthroughs by 50%. Role-based segmentation drives 20% higher activation and 15% lower churn.

The biggest gap I found: almost nobody addresses accessibility in onboarding. Focus management, keyboard navigation, screen reader support for product tours. With the EU Accessibility Act and expanding ADA enforcement, this is becoming a legal requirement, not just a UX consideration.

The handbook includes React/TypeScript code examples, a comparison of SaaS tools vs open-source libraries vs headless approaches, and a breakdown of five metrics that actually predict onboarding success.
