The median core feature adoption rate across B2B SaaS is 16.5%.

Most teams target 60-80% for their core features. That gap isn't about feature quality. It's about discoverability.

I dug into the data (Userpilot's 2024 benchmark report, n=181 companies) and wrote up what I found, including a few surprises:

→ Sales-led companies (26.7%) slightly outperform product-led ones (24.3%) on feature adoption. PLG doesn't automatically mean higher engagement.

→ HR products lead at 31%. FinTech and Healthcare sit around 22.6%. Industry context matters more than most teams realize.

→ There are 4 different formulas that produce 4 different numbers from the same data. Picking the wrong denominator (total users vs eligible users vs target users) can make a 50% adoption rate look like 20%.

The article includes TypeScript implementations for all four variants plus a React hook for tracking.
