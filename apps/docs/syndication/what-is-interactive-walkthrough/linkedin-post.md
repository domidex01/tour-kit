Every onboarding vendor uses the term "interactive walkthrough." None of them agree on what it means.

I looked it up. Every Google result is from UserGuiding, Userpilot, Whatfix, or Appcues. Screenshots of drag-and-drop editors. Zero code.

So I wrote a developer-focused definition. An interactive walkthrough is an in-app guidance pattern where each step requires the user to perform a real action — clicking a button, filling a field — to advance. Under the hood: MutationObserver, event listeners, and state gating.

The data is worth knowing: Chameleon analyzed 15 million product tour interactions. Average completion rate: 61%. Event-triggered walkthroughs are 38% more likely to complete. And tours over 10 steps see roughly 2x lower completion rates.

The biggest takeaway: step count and trigger type matter more than whether the walkthrough is "interactive" or not.
