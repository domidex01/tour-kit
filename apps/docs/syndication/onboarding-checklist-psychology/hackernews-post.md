## Title: The psychology behind onboarding checklist completion rates (19.2% average across 188 SaaS companies)

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/onboarding-checklist-psychology

## Comment to post immediately after:

I wrote this after going through Userpilot's 2025 benchmark study on checklist completion rates. The headline number (19.2% average, 10.1% median) was worse than I expected. FinTech leads at 24.5%, MarTech trails at 12.5%.

The interesting finding is that three well-documented cognitive effects predict most of the variance: the Zeigarnik effect (unfinished tasks create cognitive tension), endowed progress (pre-stamping loyalty cards nearly doubles completion), and the goal gradient (people accelerate near the finish line).

What surprised me most: smaller companies ($1-5M revenue) outperform larger ones at 27.1% completion. The likely explanation is shorter checklists and faster iteration.

The article includes React code examples using Tour Kit's checklists package. Disclosure: I'm the author of Tour Kit. But the psychology research is independent and the benchmarks come from Userpilot's data across 188 companies, not ours.
