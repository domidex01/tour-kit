## Thread (6 tweets)

**1/** The average SaaS onboarding checklist has a 19.2% completion rate.

The median is 10.1%.

4 out of 5 users who see your checklist never finish it.

But some hit 40%+. Here's the psychology behind the ones that work: 🧵

**2/** Effect #1: The Zeigarnik Effect

Your brain fixates on unfinished tasks. Incomplete orders, half-read books, unchecked boxes.

The key: make the incompleteness VISIBLE. Don't hide the checklist behind a menu. Surface it immediately.

Hidden checklist = no cognitive tension = no completion drive.

**3/** Effect #2: Endowed Progress

In a car wash study, loyalty cards with 2 pre-stamped boxes (out of 10) completed at nearly 2x the rate of blank 8-stamp cards.

Same total effort. Different psychology.

Pre-check items users already did (account creation, email verification). Start at 20%, not 0%.

**4/** Effect #3: Goal Gradient

People accelerate as they approach a goal. Clark Hull proved this in 1932.

For checklists: users are most likely to quit in the middle, most likely to push through near the end.

Keep it to 3-5 items. Beyond 5, completion drops by more than half.

**5/** The combo matters more than any single effect:

1. Show checklist immediately (Zeigarnik)
2. Pre-check 1-2 completed items (Endowed Progress)
3. 4-5 total items + progress bar (Goal Gradient)
4. Persist across sessions
