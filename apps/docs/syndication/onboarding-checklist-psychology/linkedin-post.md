The average SaaS onboarding checklist has a 19.2% completion rate.

I dug into why, using Userpilot's benchmark study across 188 SaaS companies.

Three cognitive effects separate the checklists that work (40%+ completion) from the ones that don't:

→ The Zeigarnik effect: incomplete tasks create cognitive tension. But only if the checklist is visible. Hiding it behind a menu kills the effect entirely.

→ Endowed progress: pre-checking items users already completed (account creation, email verification) nearly doubles completion in controlled studies. Start the progress bar at 20%, not 0%.

→ Goal gradient: people accelerate effort near the finish. Keep checklists at 3-5 items. Beyond 5, completion drops by more than half.

The data by industry was surprising: FinTech leads at 24.5%, MarTech trails at 12.5%. Smaller companies ($1-5M) outperform larger ones at 27.1%.
