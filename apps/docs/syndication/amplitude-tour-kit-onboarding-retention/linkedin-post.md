Your onboarding tour's completion rate is a vanity metric.

What actually matters is whether completing the tour changes user retention. Calm discovered through Amplitude's behavioral cohort analysis that retention was 3x higher among users who completed one key onboarding step. They made the step mandatory. Retention improved across the board.

I wrote a tutorial on connecting a React product tour to Amplitude's analytics: typed event schemas, step-level funnels, and a three-cohort retention analysis (completers vs. skippers vs. users who never saw the tour). The third group is your control — without it, you're comparing motivated users against everyone else.

The whole integration is about 70 lines of TypeScript. Teams that redesign onboarding flows based on this kind of drop-off data see 20-25% improvements in trial-to-paid conversion.
