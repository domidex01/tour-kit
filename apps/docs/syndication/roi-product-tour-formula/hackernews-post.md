## Title: Four formulas for measuring product tour ROI (with benchmarks)

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/roi-product-tour-formula

## Comment to post immediately after:

I wrote this after my PM asked for "the ROI of our product tours" and I realized every guide on the topic gives you one generic formula without explaining how to calculate the actual gains.

The core issue is that "product tour ROI" is actually four separate calculations: activation lift (highest confidence), churn prevention (medium confidence, needs 90 days), support ticket deflection (easiest to measure), and expansion revenue (highest value, lowest confidence).

Some numbers that surprised me during research: action-based tours show 123% higher completion than click-through tours (Chameleon's dataset of 550M interactions). Average SaaS activation rate is only 36% (Userpilot). And 70% of SaaS customers churn within the first 90 days (UserGuiding).

The biggest mistake I see teams make is attributing 100% of improvement to the tour. Tour completers are self-selected — they were probably more motivated to begin with. Discounting by 40-60% for attribution gives you a number that actually holds up when finance pushes back.

The article includes worked examples with real numbers and React code for tracking these metrics. Built on our experience with Tour Kit's analytics package, though the formulas work regardless of what tool you use.
