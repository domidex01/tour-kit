Your PM asks for the ROI of your product tours. Every guide gives you one formula: (Gains - Costs) / Costs.

Not helpful. "Gains" from product tours isn't one number — it's four:

1. Activation lift — incremental users who activated because of the tour
2. Churn prevention — revenue retained by reducing early dropout
3. Support deflection — tickets avoided (average cost: $15-25 per ticket)
4. Expansion revenue — upsells driven by feature-specific tours

The data is striking. Average SaaS activation rate is 36% (Userpilot). 70% of customers churn within 90 days (UserGuiding). Action-based tours show 123% higher completion than click-through (Chameleon, 550M interactions).

The biggest mistake: attributing 100% of improvement to the tour. Tour completers are self-selected. Use 30-50% attribution for numbers that hold up when finance pushes back.
