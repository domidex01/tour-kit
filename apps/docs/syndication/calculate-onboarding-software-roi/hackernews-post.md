## Title: The actual math behind onboarding software ROI (with build vs. buy cost data)

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/calculate-onboarding-software-roi

## Comment to post immediately after:

I put this together because I was frustrated by vendor ROI pages that claim "10x return" without showing the formula. The core finding: onboarding ROI has three measurable components (activation lift, churn reduction, support savings), and the cost side varies 100x depending on build vs buy vs open-source.

Some data points that surprised me during research:

- Building in-house costs $60K-$71K year one even for a startup. Atlassian spent $3.5M over 3 years.
- SaaS tools charge per MAU, which means costs grow non-linearly as you scale. Going from 1K to 50K MAU can turn a $299/month bill into $4K+/month.
- 3-step tours complete at 72%, but 7-step tours drop to 16%. Most teams over-build their onboarding.
- Completion rate is the wrong metric. Cohort analysis (revenue from completers vs non-completers) is what moves budget decisions.

Full disclosure: I build Tour Kit, an open-source product tour library, so I have a perspective here. The article includes numbers for all approaches (build, SaaS, open-source) with sources from Forrester, Appcues, Userpilot, Paddle, and Chameleon.
