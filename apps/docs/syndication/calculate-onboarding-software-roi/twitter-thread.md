**1/** Every onboarding vendor claims 10x ROI but none show the formula.

I pulled real cost data from Forrester, Appcues, Userpilot, and Chameleon to build an actual ROI calculator.

Here's what the numbers look like:

**2/** The formula has 3 parts:

- Activation lift: 10-point improvement = ~$35K/year (at 1K signups, $49/mo ACV)
- Churn savings: 25% reduction on $50K MRR = $75K/year
- Support: 40% fewer onboarding tickets = $24K/year

Total: $134K/year in measurable gains.

**3/** The cost side is wild:

Building in-house: $60K-$71K year one (startup scale)
Atlassian spent $3.5M over 3 years.

SaaS tools: $299/mo at 1K MAU, but $4K+/mo at 50K MAU.

Open-source: $0-$99 + dev time. No MAU scaling.

**4/** Most surprising finding: completion rate is the WRONG metric.

Chameleon's own team calls it "a lagging input metric, not a revenue metric."

The right metric: cohort analysis. Compare revenue from onboarding completers vs non-completers over 30-90 days.

**5/** Tour length benchmarks that should change how you build:

3 steps: 72% completion
5 steps: 34%
7 steps: 16%

User-triggered tours outperform auto-triggered by 2-3x.

Shorter tours, user-initiated. That's the formula.
