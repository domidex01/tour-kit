Every onboarding vendor claims 10x ROI.

None of them show the formula.

I spent a week pulling together real cost data to build an actual onboarding ROI calculator. Here's what the numbers look like for a mid-size B2B SaaS:

A 10-point activation improvement (30% to 40%) generates ~$35K/year in additional revenue at 1,000 monthly signups and $49/month ACV. Churn reduction adds another $75K. Support savings: $24K.

But the cost side is where it gets interesting.

Building in-house costs $60K-$71K in year one for a startup. Atlassian spent $3.5M over three years. SaaS tools start at $299/month but scale with MAU, hitting $4K+/month at 50K users.

The most surprising finding: completion rate is the wrong metric to track. Chameleon's own data team calls it "a lagging input metric." The real metric is cohort analysis: revenue difference between onboarding completers and non-completers over 90 days.
