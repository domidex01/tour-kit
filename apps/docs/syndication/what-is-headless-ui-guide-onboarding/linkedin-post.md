Your product tour probably doesn't match your design system.

Here's why that matters more than you think:

Most onboarding libraries ship pre-styled tooltips. Your app uses carefully chosen design tokens, spacing, and typography. Then the tour library injects its own visual language during the exact moment you're trying to build trust with a new user.

Headless UI solves this. The library handles behavior (step sequencing, focus management, keyboard navigation, accessibility). You render steps using your existing design system components. Integration drops from ~2 hours of CSS overrides to ~15 minutes.

As of April 2026, headless component adoption grew 70% YoY. AI coding tools standardized on shadcn/ui, which is built on Radix (headless). The styled-vs-headless debate is settling.

I wrote a guide covering the pattern, its evolution (HOCs to render props to hooks), and how it applies specifically to product tours.
