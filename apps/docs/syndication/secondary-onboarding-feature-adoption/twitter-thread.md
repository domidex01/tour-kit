## Thread (6 tweets)

**1/** The average SaaS feature has a 24.5% adoption rate. Median is 16.5%.

That means 3 out of 4 features you ship never get meaningful traction.

Not because they're bad. Because users never find them. 🧵

**2/** Most teams focus all their onboarding energy on the signup flow. But the real adoption gap happens after activation.

Secondary onboarding = surfacing advanced features to users who already know the basics but haven't discovered the rest of your product.

**3/** The fix is behavioral triggers, not calendar triggers.

Don't show a reporting tour "7 days after signup." Show it when the user creates their 3rd project and hasn't opened reports yet.

Gate on what users have done, not how long they've been around.

**4/** Vitaly Friedman nailed it in @smaboringmag: "Low feature adoption often has nothing to do with the feature itself, but where it sits in the UI."

Features at 25-35% adoption are your best targets. Below 20% usually means redesign, not more tooltips.
