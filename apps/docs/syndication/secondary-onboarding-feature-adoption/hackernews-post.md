## Title: 75% of SaaS features go undiscovered – behavioral triggers for post-activation onboarding

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/secondary-onboarding-feature-adoption

## Comment to post immediately after:

The average SaaS product has a core feature adoption rate of 24.5% (median 16.5%), according to Artisan Growth Strategies' 2025 benchmarks. That means three out of four features you ship never get meaningful traction.

Most onboarding content focuses on the initial signup flow. But the bigger gap is what happens after activation: surfacing advanced features to users who already understand the basics but haven't discovered the rest of the product.

I looked at how every major onboarding tool (Userpilot, Appcues, Chameleon) talks about this problem and found they all treat it as a no-code/PM concern. None show actual implementation code. The article covers behavioral trigger patterns, progressive disclosure as a React component composition problem, user segmentation without a CDP, and the TARS measurement framework from Smashing Magazine.

One finding I thought was interesting: Vitaly Friedman's point that low adoption often has nothing to do with the feature quality. It's a discoverability problem. Features that sit at 25-35% adoption are the best candidates for in-app guidance, while features below 20% probably need redesign rather than more tooltips.
