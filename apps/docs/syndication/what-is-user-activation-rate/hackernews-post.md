## Title: SaaS activation rates average 36% – what the data says about improving them

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/what-is-user-activation-rate

## Comment to post immediately after:

I pulled together benchmark data from Chameleon's 15-million product tour interaction study and Userpilot's 2025 SaaS metrics to answer a straightforward question: what actually moves user activation rates?

The most surprising finding: click-through product tours (the "click Next to continue" kind) have decent completion rates (61% average) but don't improve activation. Users advance without doing anything. Self-serve tours where users perform the actual behavior see 123% higher completion and correlate with actual activation lift.

Benchmarks vary wildly by vertical. AI/ML products sit at 54.8% (value moment is immediate), while FinTech is at 5.0% (regulatory friction before users can do anything useful). SaaS average is 36%, median 30%.

The article includes React code examples using Tour Kit (open source library I built, so full disclosure there). The code-first angle was intentional because most activation rate content is marketing-oriented with zero implementation guidance.

Sources: Chameleon benchmark report 2025, Userpilot SaaS activation data, AGL Research vertical benchmarks, Business of Apps mobile data.
