# What Is User Activation Rate? And Why Most Product Tours Don't Improve It

### The metric that separates growing SaaS from churning SaaS

*Originally published at [usertourkit.com](https://usertourkit.com/blog/what-is-user-activation-rate)*

Your signup numbers look great. Hundreds of new users per week. But 64% of them never reach the moment where your product clicks.

That gap between "signed up" and "got value" is your activation rate. It's the single metric most correlated with long-term retention. And the average SaaS product only activates 36% of signups.

## The formula

Activation Rate = (Users who completed activation event / Total signups) x 100

The activation event is product-specific. For Slack, it was 2,000 messages sent by a team. For Uber, first ride. For a project management tool, creating a project and inviting a teammate.

The trick is measuring by cohort, not all-time. All-time rates smooth out changes and hide whether your latest onboarding iteration worked.

## 2026 benchmarks

AI & ML products lead at 54.8%. SaaS average sits at 36%, median 30%. FinTech trails at 5% due to compliance friction. Mobile apps average just 8.4% globally.

If you're below your vertical's median, onboarding is where to look first.

## Why activation and adoption aren't the same thing

Activation is a single moment: the first time a user experiences core value. Adoption is sustained behavior over weeks or months. Engagement is frequency and depth.

Realistic funnel: 1,000 signups become 360 activated users. Of those, maybe 70 become regular adopters. Activation is where the biggest drop happens.

## The click-through tour problem

Chameleon analyzed 15 million product tour interactions. Average completion rate: 61%. But activation rates stay flat for many products even with tours.

The culprit: click-through tours. Users click "Next" five times without doing anything. They've watched a slideshow. The completion metric looks great. Activation doesn't move.

What works: action-based tours where users advance by performing the actual behavior. Self-serve tours achieve 123% higher completion than standard click-through tours.

## Three patterns that work

1. **Action-based progression.** Users advance by doing, not clicking. 123% higher completion.

2. **Five steps or fewer.** Top-performing tours cap here. Every additional step drops completion.

3. **Progress indicators.** +12% completion, -20% dismissals.

## The revenue math

A 25% improvement in activation yields 34% MRR growth over 12 months. Rocketbots doubled activation from 15% to 30% and saw 300% MRR growth. The numbers compound because activated users convert to paid at dramatically higher rates.

## What to do about it

Add onboarding checklists (up to 3x conversion in case studies). Switch from click-through to action-based tours. Reduce signup friction before the tour starts.
