# 6 Intercom Product Tour Alternatives That Won't Cost $273/Month

*Originally published at [usertourkit.com](https://usertourkit.com/blog/best-intercom-product-tour-alternatives)*

**Intercom product tours are expensive, limited, and bolted onto a chat platform. Here are six alternatives worth evaluating.**

Intercom product tours cost a minimum of $273 per month once you add the required add-on to a base plan. The tours themselves are limited to linear sequences, don't work on mobile, and show a median completion rate of just 34% according to Intercom's own data.

We tested six alternatives across pricing, bundle size, and developer experience. Full disclosure: Tour Kit is our project, so take our #1 ranking with appropriate skepticism.

## The short version

- **Tour Kit** (Free / $99 one-time) — Headless React library. Under 8KB gzipped. Full WCAG 2.1 AA. You own the code.
- **Appcues** ($249/mo) — No-code visual builder for product teams. Tours, modals, hotspots in one plan.
- **UserGuiding** ($174/mo) — Budget SaaS replacement. 15-minute setup. G2 rating: 4.7/5.
- **Product Fruits** ($79/mo) — Lowest-cost all-in-one. 70% cheaper than Intercom's add-on.
- **Chameleon** ($279/mo) — Enterprise pick with deep Mixpanel/Amplitude integrations.
- **Intro.js** ($9.99 one-time) — Lightweight vanilla JS library. Framework-agnostic. 13 years of maintenance.

## How to choose

Two questions narrow it down: Does your team write code or use visual builders? And do you need mobile tour support?

If you have React developers who want design system control, a headless library like Tour Kit gives you full ownership with no monthly fees. If product managers need to create tours independently, a no-code SaaS like Appcues or UserGuiding is the right call. If you just need basic step-by-step tours on any framework, Intro.js does the job for $9.99.

One angle no competitor article mentions: bundle size. Intercom loads its entire Messenger SDK even if you only use tours. For teams where Core Web Vitals matter, the JavaScript weight of your tour tool is worth measuring.
