The digital adoption platform market is now worth $1.59 billion. Every explainer is written by the vendors, for product managers.

So I wrote the developer version.

A DAP is a JavaScript overlay (100-280KB) that sits on top of your application and lets non-technical teams create walkthroughs, tooltips, and checklists through a visual editor. Pricing ranges from $55/month to $100K+/year depending on the vendor and your user count.

The question most engineering teams actually need answered: when does a DAP make sense vs building with a product tour library?

Short version: DAPs are right when PMs need to ship onboarding without developer involvement. Libraries are right when your engineering team owns the experience and bundle size matters.

The full article covers vendor pricing, architecture tradeoffs, a comparison matrix, and a decision framework.
