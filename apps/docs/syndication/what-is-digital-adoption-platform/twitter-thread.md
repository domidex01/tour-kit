**2/** How DAPs work technically:

A JS agent (100-280KB) loads on every page, reads the DOM, and renders an overlay. Content lives on the vendor's servers. PMs author via WYSIWYG. Your team doesn't touch the code.

That's the feature. It's also the limitation.

**3/** DAP pricing in April 2026:

- HelpHero: $55/mo (1K MAUs)
- ProductFruits: $149/mo (1.5K MAUs)
- UserGuiding: $249/mo
- Userpilot: $299/mo (2K MAUs)
- WalkMe/Whatfix: custom ($30K-$100K+/yr)

All MAU-based = costs scale with users.

**4/** DAP vs product tour library:

- DAP: 100KB+ JS overlay, no-code editor, $55-$299+/mo
- Library: 5-15KB gzipped, code-first, free or one-time

The real question: who owns the onboarding experience — your PM team or your engineering team?

**5/** Choose a DAP when non-technical teams need to create guidance without devs.

Choose a library when your engineers own onboarding, bundle size matters, and you'd rather pay once.

Choose Tour Kit when you want DAP-level features as 10 composable npm packages at <8KB core.
