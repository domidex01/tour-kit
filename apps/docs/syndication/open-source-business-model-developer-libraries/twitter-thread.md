## Thread (6 tweets)

**1/** The open-source services market hit $49 billion in 2025. But most of that money flows to companies with 50+ engineers.

If you're a solo dev shipping a React library, the playbook looks completely different. Here's what I learned choosing a business model.

**2/** There are really only 3 models that work for developer libraries:

- Open core (MIT core + paid extensions)
- Dual licensing (AGPL + commercial)
- Open-source SaaS (free code, paid hosting)

Sponsorship? That's a tip, not a business model.

**3/** Open core wins for most libraries. The key insight from @opencoreventures:

Segment by BUYER, not by effort.

Features for individual devs = free.
Features for VPs and product teams = paid.

"How hard was this to build?" is the wrong question.

**4/** The tooling finally caught up.

@polar_sh: license keys + payments for 4% + 40c
@GitHubSponsors: 0% fee on personal sponsorships

Two years ago this meant building a custom Stripe integration. Now it's a weekend setup.

**5/** My 5 rules after going through this:

1. Free tier must be genuinely complete
2. Never reclassify free features as paid
3. Price for the buyer, not the builder
4. License validation at build time, NEVER runtime
5. Open source your business decisions too
