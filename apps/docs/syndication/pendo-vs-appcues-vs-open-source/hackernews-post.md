## Title: Pendo ($48K/yr median) vs Appcues ($879/mo) vs open-source libraries for product tours

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/pendo-vs-appcues-vs-open-source

## Comment to post immediately after:

I've been researching the actual economics of product tour tooling and the numbers surprised me.

Pendo's median contract sits at $48,463/year according to Vendr marketplace data from 127 real purchases. Their startup program is $7K/year but graduates to $25K-$35K after Series B. Appcues starts at $249/month but at 10,000 MAU you're looking at $18K-$24K/year before add-ons.

Meanwhile, open-source React libraries (React Joyride, Shepherd.js, Driver.js, Tour Kit) ship at 5-50KB and cost $0. The tradeoff is no visual builder — every tour change goes through code.

I built Tour Kit (one of the open-source options listed), so I'm biased. But the pricing data is all from public sources — Vendr, Featurebase, Userorbit, and the vendors' own pricing pages. Happy to discuss the methodology or where I got the numbers wrong.
