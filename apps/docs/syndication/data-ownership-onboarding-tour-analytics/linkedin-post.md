Most SaaS onboarding tools say "you own your data."

Then you try to leave, and you discover what that actually means.

I spent a week reading through Pendo, Appcues, and WalkMe's data processing agreements, export capabilities, and retention policies. The gap between the marketing promise and the operational reality is significant.

A few findings:

WalkMe retains personal data for up to 7 years and processes deletion requests within 90 days. GDPR gives you 30 days to respond to subject access requests. If your vendor can't keep up, the compliance failure is yours.

The EU Data Act (enforced September 2025) now requires machine-readable exports at no cost. As of April 2026, no major onboarding vendor has publicly documented their compliance.

The honest counterargument: SOC 2 Type II certification costs $50,000-$100,000. Vendors amortize that across thousands of customers. Self-hosting analytics isn't free either.

The question isn't whether SaaS onboarding is always wrong. It's whether your team has made a deliberate choice about where tour analytics live — or defaulted into vendor custody because the tool was already in the stack.
