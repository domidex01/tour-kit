Linear product tours have a 19.2% average completion rate. Your users click "Next" until it stops and retain almost nothing.

Contextual onboarding is the alternative. Instead of a fixed sequence, you trigger guidance based on what the user is actually doing — a tooltip when they visit a feature for the first time, a checklist item after they complete a prerequisite, a nudge when they hit a usage threshold.

NNG calls traditional tours "push revelations." Contextual onboarding is "pull revelations" — help that shows up when the user needs it, not when your tour sequence says so.

The results: 20–30% fewer support tickets (Chameleon), 16–21% reduction in documentation lookups (Whatfix), and no single "completion rate" to worry about because each piece of guidance stands on its own.
