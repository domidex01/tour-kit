## Title: The Real Cost of Pendo for a Series A Startup

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/pendo-cost-startup

## Comment to post immediately after:

I've been researching onboarding tool pricing while building an open-source alternative, and was surprised by how much the "Pendo for Startups" program obscures the real cost trajectory.

The startup program is $7K/year, but it expires when you raise past Series A. Standard Base tier starts at $15.9K and goes to $35K depending on MAU volume. The median customer across all tiers pays $48.5K/year (Vendr data). Adding professional services ($5-15K), training, and renewal escalation (5-20% annually), a realistic 3-year TCO for a growing startup lands around $85K.

The part I found most interesting: most startups already run Mixpanel, Amplitude, or PostHog for product analytics. Pendo bundles analytics into every tier, which means you're paying $25-50K/year for duplicate analytics capability when you really just need the in-app guides.

All pricing data is sourced from Vendr marketplace data, Featurebase's customer reports, and UserGuiding's pricing analysis. Disclosure: I'm building Tour Kit, an open-source React tour library, so I have an obvious angle — but the numbers are independently verifiable.
