Pendo for Startups costs $7,000/year. The 3-year total cost of ownership? $85,000.

I broke down Pendo's real pricing for Series A startups, and the gap between the sticker price and actual cost is larger than most engineering leaders expect:

- The startup program expires when you raise past Series A
- Standard Base tier jumps to $15.9K-$35K (customer reports confirm $35K quotes)
- Professional services, training, and renewal escalation add 40-70% on top
- Most startups already pay for analytics separately — Pendo bundles duplicate capability into every tier

The median Pendo customer pays $48,463/year (Vendr data). For a Series A company allocating 5-20% of budget to tooling, that's a significant line item for one onboarding tool.

A composable open-source stack — your existing analytics + an MIT-licensed tour library — runs about $4,500 over the same three years.

Where does Pendo still make sense? Enterprise scale (50K+ MAU), teams with no frontend engineering, and orgs consolidating 3+ tools into one vendor.
