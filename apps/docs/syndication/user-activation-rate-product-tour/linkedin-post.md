Most product teams celebrate tour completion rates while activation stays flat.

After analyzing benchmark data from 550M in-app interactions (Chameleon 2025), the pattern is clear: the tours that move activation look nothing like the tours that inflate completion numbers.

Three findings that changed how I think about onboarding:

1. Action-based tours (users advance by performing real actions) show 123% higher completion than click-through tours. And that completion actually predicts activation.

2. Four steps is the sweet spot (74% completion). Seven or more steps collapse to 16%. The drop isn't gradual.

3. Role-based personalization improved activation by 47% in one study. Different users have different activation events — they need different tours.

The average SaaS activation rate is 36% (Userpilot 2025). A 25% improvement in that number yields 34% MRR growth over 12 months.
