**2/** Stars are a discovery mechanism, not a revenue signal.

GitHub's search results weight star counts. More stars = higher ranking = more organic discovery = more stars.

For a solo dev with no marketing budget, that flywheel is the real business value. Not the number itself.

**3/** The correlation between stars and npm downloads? Moderate at best for JavaScript. Weak for everything else.

The correlation between stars and revenue? Almost nonexistent without a separate monetization strategy.

**4/** OpenSSL secures virtually every HTTPS connection on the internet.

Annual donations: ~$2,000.

One dev put a donation link on their 5K+ star repo. Result: one recurring donation. $1/month.

Stars don't translate to money on their own.

**5/** Projects that turned stars into revenue share one pattern:

They treated open source as a distribution channel, not a business model.

Postiz (open-source social media tool): $14,200/month MRR as a solo dev. Revenue from cloud hosting, not the code.

**6/** The metric that actually matters: time to second interaction.

Someone who stars today and opens an issue tomorrow > 100 people who starred and disappeared.

Track downstream signals (npm installs, doc page views, issue quality), not the star counter.
