Most product tour libraries were built for desktop. On mobile, they break.

96% of internet users are on mobile devices. 49% hold their phone one-handed. 75% of all touch interactions are thumb-driven. Yet most onboarding tooltips still anchor to the top of the screen where thumbs can't reach.

I wrote a guide on the touch-friendly patterns that fix this:

- Thumb zone layouts that keep navigation in the bottom third of the screen
- Bottom sheets instead of anchored tooltips for step content
- WCAG 2.2 touch target sizing (44px AAA, not the 24px AA minimum)
- Lazy loading and portal rendering to protect Core Web Vitals

The most interesting finding: Chameleon's analysis of 15M product tour interactions shows three-step tours achieve 72% completion — the highest of any tour length. On mobile, shorter always wins.
