**1/** 96% of internet users are on mobile, but most product tour libraries were designed for 1440px desktop screens. Here's what actually works for mobile-first tours:

**2/** Thumb zone layout: 49% of users hold their phone one-handed. 75% of interactions are thumb-driven. Put your Next/Back/Skip buttons at the bottom of the screen, not anchored to the tooltip. That one change transforms usability.

**3/** Bottom sheets > tooltips on mobile. When a tooltip anchors to a nav item at the top of the screen, users have to stretch into the "hard zone" to tap it. Bottom sheets keep everything reachable while highlighting the target element.

**4/** Touch targets: WCAG 2.2 AA says 24px minimum, but Apple HIG, Material Design, and the MIT Touch Lab all converge on 44px. Users with motor impairments see 75% higher error rates on small targets. 44px is the real floor.

**5/** The surprising stat: Chameleon analyzed 15M tour interactions and found 3-step tours hit 72% completion — the highest of any length. On mobile, shorter wins. If you need 8 steps, split into contextual micro-tours triggered by user actions.
