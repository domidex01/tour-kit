# How to actually evaluate onboarding tools (beyond G2 star ratings)

### We tested 10 tools and found three things review platforms consistently miss

*Originally published at [usertourkit.com](https://usertourkit.com/blog/best-onboarding-tool-reviews)*

Capterra lists 707 onboarding products with over 14,000 published reviews. Sounds comprehensive. But most of those 707 products are HR and employee onboarding tools, not the product tour and in-app guidance tools that software teams actually need.

The product tour category lives under G2's "Digital Adoption Platform" label. We pulled scores from both platforms for the 10 tools that product and engineering teams actually evaluate, installed the open-source libraries, and cross-referenced review patterns.

Here's what we found.

## The ratings, at a glance

UserGuiding leads G2 at 4.7/5. Product Fruits leads Capterra at 4.8/5. Chameleon has earned G2 Leader status for five consecutive seasons through Spring 2026. Pendo and Chameleon tie at 4.4/5 on G2.

But the real story is what's missing from these ratings.

## Three things review platforms don't capture

**1. Open-source tools are completely absent.** Shepherd.js has 13,000 GitHub stars. React Joyride has 7,600. Driver.js is actively maintained with MIT licensing. None of them have G2 or Capterra profiles. If your engineering team makes build-vs-buy decisions based on G2 scores, you're evaluating with half the options missing.

**2. Accessibility isn't tested.** We searched both platforms for onboarding tools mentioning WCAG, ARIA, or keyboard navigation. Zero results. For teams building products that must meet accessibility standards, the review platforms offer no signal at all.

**3. Technical impact isn't measured.** Not a single G2 review discusses bundle size, Core Web Vitals impact, or JavaScript payload. The metrics that matter to developers are entirely absent from the metrics that matter to review platforms.

## The pricing reality

The Capterra average for onboarding software entry pricing is $79/month. But that average hides enormous variance. Open-source libraries like React Joyride and Driver.js are free. UserGuiding starts at $69/month with published pricing. Pendo, Appcues, and Chameleon all hide behind "contact sales."

Pricing transparency itself is a differentiator. When three of the seven commercial tools on our list won't show you a price until you talk to sales, the tools that do publish pricing earn trust by default.

## How to choose

If your team has React developers and a design system, consider a headless library that gives you code ownership. If your product team needs to build tours without engineering tickets, a SaaS platform like Appcues or UserGuiding makes more sense despite the monthly cost. If you need product analytics bundled with in-app guidance, enterprise platforms like Pendo are the category, with enterprise contracts to match.
