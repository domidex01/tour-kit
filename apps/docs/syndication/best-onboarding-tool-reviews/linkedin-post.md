G2 and Capterra are useful for evaluating onboarding tools. But they have a blind spot that most buyers don't notice.

I pulled review data for 10 onboarding and product tour tools across both platforms. Three findings surprised me:

Open-source libraries with combined 20K+ GitHub stars (Shepherd.js, React Joyride, Driver.js) don't exist on either review platform. PMs evaluating tools on G2 are making decisions with half the options hidden.

Accessibility isn't mentioned once. I searched both platforms for WCAG, ARIA, or keyboard navigation in onboarding tool reviews. Zero results. For teams building accessible products, these review platforms offer no signal.

Pricing transparency varies wildly. UserGuiding publishes pricing at $69/month. Appcues, Pendo, Chameleon all require a sales call. The Capterra average is $79/month, but that hides enormous variance between free open-source and five-figure enterprise contracts.
