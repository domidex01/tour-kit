## Title: Why YC startups should skip SaaS onboarding tools

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/yc-startups-skip-saas-onboarding-tools

## Comment to post immediately after:

I built Tour Kit, an open-source React library for product tours, so my bias is obvious. But I ran the numbers on this because the build-vs-buy advice for onboarding is almost always written by the SaaS vendors themselves.

The core argument: Appcues starts at $249/month, Userpilot at $299/month. Both bill per MAU. A YC startup with $500K, 12-18 months of runway, and two engineers who write React daily is paying enterprise rates for a problem their team can solve in a morning with an open-source library.

Three data points drove this piece: (1) 66% of YC W24 was AI-integrated, meaning these are technical teams where the "non-technical people need a visual builder" value prop doesn't apply. (2) Retool's 2026 Build vs. Buy Report found 35% of enterprises have already replaced SaaS with custom builds. (3) MAU-based pricing means your onboarding costs scale against your success.

The honest counterpoint: if your team has zero frontend engineers or a non-technical growth lead owns onboarding, SaaS removes a real bottleneck. I tried to steelman that case fairly in the article.

Curious what other YC founders/early-stage teams actually use for onboarding. Are people building from scratch, using libraries, or paying for SaaS at the seed stage?
