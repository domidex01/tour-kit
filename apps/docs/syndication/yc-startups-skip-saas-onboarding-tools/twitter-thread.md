**1/** YC startups: stop paying $300/month for onboarding SaaS tools.

Your team writes React. You have 12 months of runway. The math doesn't work.

I ran the numbers. Here's what I found:

**2/** Appcues Essentials: $2,988/year

All four get you to the same place: a 5-step onboarding tour. The SaaS tools are 1-2 hours faster to set up. The library costs $3,000+ less per year.

**3/** The real problem isn't the $300/month. It's that every SaaS onboarding tool bills per MAU.

Grow from 2K to 10K users? Your bill doubles.
Grow to 50K? You're negotiating enterprise contracts for tooltip positioning.

Libraries don't scale against you.

**4/** 66% of YC W24 was AI-integrated. These are technical teams.

The entire value prop of onboarding SaaS is "non-technical people can build tours without engineering."

If your founders write code, you're paying for a problem you don't have.

**5/** The honest counterpoint: if nobody on your team writes frontend code, or a non-technical growth lead owns onboarding, SaaS removes a real bottleneck.

Not every startup should avoid it. But most YC teams in 2026 should.
