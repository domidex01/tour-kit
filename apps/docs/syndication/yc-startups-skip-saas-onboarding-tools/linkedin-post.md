SaaS onboarding tools charge $249-$879/month. For a YC startup with $500K and two engineers, that's $3,000-$10,000/year on tooltip editors.

I ran the numbers on five approaches to user onboarding at seed stage:

- Appcues: $2,988/year (scales with MAU)
- Userpilot: $3,588/year (scales with MAU)
- Open-source library: $0 + 4-8 hours of setup
- Build from scratch: $8,000-15,000 (don't do this)

The pattern is clear: technical teams that already write React are paying for a capability they don't need. The whole value prop of onboarding SaaS is that non-technical people can build tours without engineering. If your founders write code, that's solving a problem you don't have.

Retool's 2026 Build vs. Buy Report confirms the trend: 35% of enterprises have already replaced at least one SaaS tool with custom software. AI-assisted development is accelerating this further.

The counterpoint is real: if nobody on your team writes frontend code, SaaS removes a genuine bottleneck. But for most YC teams in 2026, the library path saves $15K-$40K over three years.
