60-70% of annual SaaS churn happens in the first 90 days. The onboarding window isn't just important. It's the entire ballgame.

I researched how Slack, Notion, Dropbox, and Canva connect their product tours to the user actions that predict retention. The core finding: there's a critical distinction between the aha moment (emotional realization) and the activation event (behavioral action that correlates with retention).

Most product tours target features. The ones that reduce churn target the activation event.

Some numbers from the research:
- 3-step tours: 72% completion rate
- 7-step tours: 16% completion
- User-triggered tours outperform auto-popups by 123%
- Slack's activation rate: 93%

The Reddit case study is particularly interesting. They assumed joining a subreddit was the aha moment, but comment participation is the actual retention predictor. Their onboarding guides users to the wrong action.

I put together a 4-layer framework for mapping tours directly to activation events, with code examples and a comparison table across 7 products.
