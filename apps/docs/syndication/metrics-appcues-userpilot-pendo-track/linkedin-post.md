Every onboarding tool says it has "powerful analytics."

I tested what Appcues, Userpilot, and Pendo actually track. The results were surprising.

Appcues only measures events inside its own flows. Userpilot has decent funnels and cohorts but the dashboard freezes under load. Pendo is genuinely strong (autocapture, retroactive queries) but costs a median $48K/year.

The bigger insight: none of them track survey fatigue across a user's lifecycle, cross-mechanism correlation, complete AARRR/HEART metrics, or their own performance impact on Core Web Vitals.

If your team already runs Amplitude or PostHog, piping 10 custom events from your onboarding code might be more useful than paying for a second analytics layer.
