The median product tour completion rate is 34%.

Most product teams see that and think: "How do we get it higher?"

Wrong question.

Completion rate tells you whether someone clicked through your tour. It doesn't tell you whether they learned the feature you were teaching. A tour that hides the Skip button has high completion and zero learning.

The right A/B test metric is the downstream activation event. "Created first dashboard within 24 hours" instead of "Finished the tour."

57% of leaders say onboarding friction directly impacts revenue (OnRamp 2026 report). That makes your product tour one of the highest-impact UX surfaces in your product, and one of the least tested.

Two findings that surprised me while researching this:

1. Checking A/B test results daily inflates false-positive rates from 5% to 30% (the peeking problem)
2. No existing guide addresses WCAG 2.1 AA compliance during A/B testing of onboarding tours
