70% of users skip product tours.

But here's what the data actually shows: forcing users through the tour produces similar churn to letting them skip. Forced completion ≠ retention.

I dug into the dismissal data and found the real opportunity isn't in preventing skips — it's in what happens after.

Three patterns that changed our approach:

→ Snooze with step persistence (resume at step 4, not step 1)
→ Dismissal reason tracking ("too long" vs "already know this" — no library captures this)
→ Contextual re-engagement (show a single hint when the user encounters the feature the skipped step covered)

The most overlooked part: accessibility. Escape must dismiss, focus must return to the trigger element, and screen readers need an announcement. Most libraries get this wrong.
