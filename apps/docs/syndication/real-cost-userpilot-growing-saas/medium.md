# The Real Cost of Userpilot for Growing SaaS Teams

## That $299/month price tag is just the beginning

Userpilot's pricing page shows $299/month for the Starter plan. That number is accurate for about five minutes. Specifically, the five minutes before your SaaS crosses 2,000 monthly active users and you discover what "Growth" pricing actually means.

We built Tour Kit, an open-source onboarding library, so we're biased. Every number below comes from Userpilot's own pricing page, Vendr's transaction database, G2 reviews, or Capterra listings.

## The pricing cliff at 2,001 MAU

Userpilot has a hard step function at 2,000 monthly active users. Below that threshold, you're on the Starter plan at $299/month ($3,588/year). Hit 2,001 MAU and you jump to the Growth plan at $799/month ($9,588/year). That's a $6,000 annual increase triggered by a single user.

Most B2B SaaS products cross 2,000 MAU within 6-12 months of reaching product-market fit. If you're evaluating Userpilot because your user base is growing, you're probably evaluating the wrong plan.

According to Vendr's transaction data, actual Userpilot spend ranges from $7,638 to $60,680 annually. An 8x spread.

## What the advertised price doesn't include

Several features that growing teams need are either plan-gated or add-ons not included in the headline price:

- A/B testing for flows: requires Growth ($799/month minimum)
- Custom analytics and event-based reporting: requires Growth
- Session replays: add-on pricing not published
- Salesforce/HubSpot CRM sync: add-on or Enterprise only
- Data retention: only 1 year on Starter (3 years on Growth)
- Mobile support: web-only as of April 2026

One G2 reviewer wrote: "The pricing model becomes prohibitive as you scale. We started at $299 and within a year were looking at quotes above $15K."

## The 3-year total cost of ownership

For a team growing from 1,000 to 5,000 MAU, the 3-year total comes to over $38,000. That's the subscription plus implementation hours, mobile tooling gaps, and session replay add-ons. It doesn't account for MAU overage charges, additional seats, or CRM integration costs.

The $299/month headline turned into $38,000+ over three years.

## When Userpilot is worth it

To be fair, Userpilot isn't overpriced for every team. It makes sense when your product team is non-technical and needs a visual flow builder, when you need NPS/CSAT/surveys bundled into one platform, when you're above 10,000 MAU with enterprise budget, or when you don't have React developers on staff.

It doesn't make sense when you're a developer-led team writing React, when you're in the 2,000-5,000 MAU dead zone paying enterprise rates for startup-stage growth, or when you need mobile onboarding that Userpilot doesn't offer.

## The alternative math

Tour Kit costs $99 once. No monthly fees, no MAU tracking. The tradeoff: you need React developers and there's no visual builder. For a team building standard product tours, implementation takes 8-16 hours at $150/hr. That's $1,200-$2,400 in developer time plus $99 for the license, versus $9,588+ for Userpilot Growth in the same 12-month period.

The deeper question isn't "how much does Userpilot cost?" It's "do you want your onboarding cost to scale with your success?" Every MAU-based tool answers "yes." Code-owned tools answer "no."
