## Title: The Real Cost of Userpilot for Growing SaaS Teams

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/real-cost-userpilot-growing-saas

## Comment to post immediately after:

Author here. We build an open-source onboarding library (Tour Kit), so take this analysis with appropriate skepticism regarding our recommendations at the end.

The core finding that's framework-agnostic: Userpilot has a hard pricing cliff at 2,001 MAU where costs jump $6K/year. Above that, pricing is entirely opaque. Vendr's transaction data shows actual annual spend ranging from $7,638 to $60,680 for the same product category. The 8x spread is the part that surprised us.

We also mapped the features that aren't included in the advertised price: A/B testing, custom analytics, session replays, and CRM sync are all plan-gated or add-on. Data retention on the Starter plan is only 1 year.

The 3-year TCO for a team growing from 1K-5K MAU came to $38,000+ once you add implementation, mobile tooling gaps (Userpilot is web-only), and add-ons.

This applies to the broader SaaS pricing transparency conversation. Per-MAU pricing models in general create a cost structure that scales with customer success. Whether that's a feature or a bug depends on who's writing the check.

Happy to discuss the methodology or the numbers.
