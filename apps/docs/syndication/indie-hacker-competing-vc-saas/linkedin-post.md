VC-backed ones? 42.8%.

The funded companies are actually growing slower. (ChartMogul SaaS Growth Report)

I've been building a developer tool as a solo founder, competing against incumbents with $40M+ in funding. The conventional wisdom says I shouldn't bother. The data says otherwise.

Three things that surprised me:

→ Bootstrapped companies spend $5K per customer acquisition vs $21K for VC-backed (SaaS Capital 2025). That 4x efficiency gap compounds every quarter.

→ Micro-niches grew 340% compared to broad platforms. VC investors need 10x returns, so $3M ARR is a "failure." For a solo founder, it's life-changing money.

→ 70% of SaaS companies have unacceptable churn levels. Venture funding masks the problem. When the money runs out, so does the company.

The honest counterargument: VC money buys hiring speed, market awareness, and infrastructure capacity. If your product needs a big team or managed backend, bootstrapping is painful.

But for developer tools where users can npm install and evaluate in 20 minutes? The indie hacker has structural advantages funded companies can't copy.

HBR's December 2025 study found 82% of bootstrapped founders report higher satisfaction than VC-backed counterparts.
