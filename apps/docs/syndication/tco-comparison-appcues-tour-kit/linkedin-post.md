Most teams evaluate onboarding tools on year-one cost. That hides the real story.

We modeled the 3-year total cost of ownership for Appcues vs Tour Kit (our open-source React library) at three MAU tiers. The results:

At 5K MAU: $54K vs $26K (53% savings)
At 25K MAU: $114K vs $26K (78% savings)
At 100K MAU: $264K+ vs $36K (86% savings)

The compounding happens because MAU-based pricing scales against the thing you're trying to achieve (user growth), while a client-side library has zero per-user cost.

We were transparent about where Appcues wins: teams without frontend engineers, PMs who need to ship in hours, and products under 2,500 MAU that plan to stay there.

But for engineering-led teams above 10K MAU, the 3-year savings from switching to code-owned onboarding typically fund 2-3 months of engineering time — with money left over.
