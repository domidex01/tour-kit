## Title: TCO Comparison: 3 Years of Appcues vs. an Open-Source Product Tour Library

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/tco-comparison-appcues-tour-kit

## Comment to post immediately after:

I built Tour Kit, an open-source headless product tour library for React, so my bias here is transparent. But the numbers are all sourced from public pricing pages and Vendr contract data.

The core finding: at 5K MAU, Appcues costs roughly 2x an open-source approach over 3 years ($54K vs $26K). At 25K MAU it's 4.5x ($114K vs $26K). At 100K MAU it's 7x+ ($264K vs $36K). The gap compounds because MAU-based pricing scales against you while client-side library costs stay flat.

The model assumes a US senior React developer at $150/hr, which is the most expensive scenario for the open-source path. If your team is in a lower cost-of-living region, the gap widens further.

I tried to be honest about where Appcues wins: zero frontend engineers, need something live in hours, or staying under 2,500 MAU permanently. The visual builder has genuine value for non-technical teams.

Curious whether others have done similar TCO analysis for other SaaS-vs-OSS categories — the compounding effect of per-user pricing plus annual increases seems like it should show up in any category with usage-based pricing.
