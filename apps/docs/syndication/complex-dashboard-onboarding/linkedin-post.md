62% of new signups never came back after their first day on our analytics dashboard.

The problem wasn't the product. It was the onboarding.

We were running a 12-step tooltip tour that pointed at every widget. Completion rates collapsed past step 5. Users who did finish retained almost nothing.

What moved the needle: replacing the feature dump with 3-step role-based tours. Different roles (ops, analytics, finance) got different tours targeting their specific workflow. 7-day retention jumped 34%.

Three patterns that work for complex dashboards:

1. Role-based routing — one signup question to branch the entire onboarding path
2. Empty state onboarding — use the blank dashboard as your primary teaching surface
3. Everboarding — contextual micro-tours triggered by behavior, not calendar dates

Working memory holds ~7 items for 30 seconds. Only 20% of users read page content. Dashboard onboarding has to work within those constraints.
