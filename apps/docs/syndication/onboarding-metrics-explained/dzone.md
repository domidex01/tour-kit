# Onboarding metrics explained: every KPI with formulas

Most SaaS teams measure signups and churn. Everything between those two numbers is onboarding, and most teams have no visibility into it.

As of April 2026, 59% of SaaS buyers regret at least one software purchase made in the past 18 months (Gartner). That regret crystallizes during onboarding, the first 7 to 30 days where users decide if your product is worth keeping.

This guide covers every onboarding metric worth tracking, organized into four categories.

## Activation metrics

**User activation rate:** (Users who completed activation event / Total new signups) x 100. Average: 36% (Userpilot).

**Time to value:** Timestamp of activation event minus timestamp of first login. Cross-industry average across 547 companies: 1 day, 12 hours, 23 minutes (Userpilot).

**Feature adoption rate:** (Users who used feature / Total active users) x 100. Core features should target 80%+.

## Engagement metrics

**Tour completion rate:** (Users who finished / Users who started) x 100. Average: 61% (Chameleon, 15M interactions).

**DAU/MAU ratio:** (Daily Active Users / Monthly Active Users) x 100. SaaS median: 13%.

**Retention curves:** Week N retention = (Users active in week N / Original cohort) x 100. Products with structured onboarding retain 2.6x more at week 4 (Appcues).

## Satisfaction metrics

**NPS:** % Promoters minus % Detractors. SaaS median: ~31.

**CSAT:** (Satisfied responses / Total responses) x 100. Best for specific touchpoints.

**CES:** Sum of scores / Number of responses. Strongest loyalty predictor (Gartner).

## Business impact metrics

**ROI:** (Revenue impact minus Total cost) / Total cost x 100.

**Trial-to-paid:** Free trial (no card): 8-12% median, 15-20% with guided onboarding. That's a 40-60% improvement.
