# Every onboarding KPI explained (with the formulas nobody includes)

*A practical reference for product and engineering teams measuring what happens between signup and retention*

*Originally published at [usertourkit.com](https://usertourkit.com/blog/onboarding-metrics-explained)*

---

Most SaaS teams measure signups and churn. Everything between those two numbers is onboarding, and most teams fly blind through it.

As of April 2026, 59% of SaaS buyers regret at least one software purchase made in the past 18 months (Gartner). That regret almost always crystallizes during onboarding, the first 7 to 30 days where users decide if your product is worth keeping.

Here's every onboarding metric worth tracking, organized into four categories, with the actual formula for each.

## Activation metrics: did users reach value?

**User activation rate** is the single most important onboarding metric. The average SaaS activation rate is 36% (Userpilot). That means nearly two-thirds of signups leave without experiencing core value.

Formula: (Users who completed activation event / Total new signups) x 100

**Time to value (TTV)** measures elapsed time between first login and core benefit. The cross-industry average across 547 SaaS companies is 1 day, 12 hours, and 23 minutes (Userpilot).

**Feature adoption rate** tracks what percentage of active users engage with a specific feature. Core features should hit 80%+ adoption. Below 40% means your onboarding isn't surfacing it effectively.

## Engagement metrics: are users coming back?

**Tour completion rate** averages 61% across 15 million interactions (Chameleon). But what matters more is *where* users drop off, not the topline number.

**DAU/MAU ratio** (stickiness) measures the percentage of monthly users who return daily. SaaS median: 13%. Top quartile: 20%+. Only 12% of SaaS users rate their onboarding as "effective" (UserGuiding).

**Retention curves** track users at week 1 (habit signal), week 4 (workflow integration), and week 12 (long-term stickiness). Products with structured onboarding retain 2.6x more users at week 4 (Appcues).

## Satisfaction metrics: how do users feel?

Three survey types dominate:

**NPS** (Net Promoter Score): % Promoters minus % Detractors. Best for tracking trends over time.

**CSAT** (Customer Satisfaction): satisfied responses / total responses x 100. Best for evaluating specific touchpoints.

**CES** (Customer Effort Score): average of all scores on a 1-7 scale. The strongest predictor of customer loyalty, stronger than CSAT or NPS (Gartner).

## Business impact metrics: what's the revenue impact?

**Onboarding ROI**: (Revenue impact - Total cost) / Total cost x 100. Revenue impact breaks down into activation lift, churn prevention, support savings, and expansion revenue.

**Trial-to-paid conversion** is directly influenced by onboarding quality. Free trials without card: 8-12% median, 15-20% with guided onboarding. That's a consistent 40-60% improvement.

## The decision framework

Not every team needs every metric. Pre-PMF: focus on activation rate and TTV. Post-PMF: add retention curves and trial-to-paid. At scale: layer in DAU/MAU, CES, and ROI calculations.
