# What Is Time to Value? The SaaS Metric Your Dashboard Is Missing

### Why onboarding completion rate lies, and what to measure instead

*Originally published at [usertourkit.com](https://usertourkit.com/blog/what-is-time-to-value)*

Your onboarding completion rate sits at 78%. Half those users still churn within 30 days. The number you should be watching instead is time to value.

## The definition

Time to value (TTV) is the elapsed time between a user's first interaction with your product and the moment they experience its core benefit.

The formula: `TTV = timestamp(first_value_event) - timestamp(signup)`

Unlike vanity metrics such as "logged in" or "visited dashboard," TTV tracks whether the user reached the outcome your product exists to deliver. As of April 2026, the average TTV across 547 SaaS companies is 1 day, 12 hours, and 23 minutes (Userpilot Benchmark Report).

"First value event" is product-specific. At Instacart, CPO Daniel Danker found that "customers that place an order for perishable items are more likely to come back on a recurring basis" (Product School). The value event there is a perishable-item order, not just any purchase.

## There are actually seven types

Most articles list three or four. Here are all seven:

**Immediate (ITTV)** — Value on first use. Currency converter, spell checker.

**Short (STTV)** — Results within hours. Squarespace publishing a site in one session.

**Long (LTTV)** — Weeks or months. Enterprise BI, ERP systems.

**Basic (TTBV)** — Foundational benefit hit. CRM: sending first email campaign.

**Exceed (TTEV)** — Benefits surpass expectations. Slack improving culture beyond messaging.

**Perceived (PTTV)** — How fast value *feels*. Progress bars, skeleton screens, and guided tours compress the feeling of waiting without changing actual elapsed time.

**Recurring (RTTV)** — Each use cycle has its own value moment. Fitness apps delivering results every workout.

## The benchmarks that matter

- Overall SaaS average: 1 day 12 hours (Userpilot, 547 companies)
- CRM/sales: 1 day 4–5 hours
- FinTech: ~1.7 days
- HR Tech: ~3.8 days
- Top PLG products targeting 2026: under 60 seconds (ProductLed)

The gap between "under 60 seconds" and "3.8 days" is where onboarding design earns its keep.

## TTV vs. activation rate vs. aha moment

**Activation rate** is binary — did the user finish a set of steps? Average: 37.5% across SaaS.

**Aha moment** is emotional — "oh, this is why I signed up." Hard to timestamp.

**TTV** bridges the two. It puts a measurable duration on the journey from signup to value. As Product School puts it: "TTV is a practical bridge metric. It helps product teams prove their work drives real business impact."

## Four ways to reduce it

1. Remove signup friction (Dropbox cut to email-only).
2. Guide users to the value event with product tours and checklists.
3. Use progressive disclosure — show only what matters now.
4. Let AI personalize the path (Shopify's Sidekick collapsed TTV from days to minutes).

HubSpot invested in guided onboarding and saw 23% customer growth. Improving activation by 25% can drive a 34% revenue boost.
