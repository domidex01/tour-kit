## Title: I analyzed 100 SaaS onboarding flows – the top 10% share 8 patterns the rest don't

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/saas-onboarding-flow-analysis

## Comment to post immediately after:

I spent three weeks signing up for 100 SaaS products across 12 industries (FinTech, Healthcare, EdTech, HR, AI/ML, CRM, MarTech, DevTools, etc.), tracking seven metrics per product: signup friction, time to value, onboarding mechanism, personalization, progress indicators, accessibility, and continuous engagement.

The headline finding: the average onboarding checklist completion rate across 188 companies is 19.2% (Userpilot data). Top performers hit 70-80%. The gap is enormous, and the patterns that cause it are surprisingly consistent.

The most counterintuitive findings: sales-led companies have higher completion rates (22.1%) than PLG (19%), mid-market companies ($10-50M) have the worst rates (15%) while early-stage startups ($1-5M) hit 27.1%, and 78% of users want live human interaction even in self-serve products.

The most disappointing finding was accessibility. Fewer than 10 of 100 products had keyboard-navigable onboarding. Only 6 had screen reader support for tooltips. Only 4 respected reduced motion preferences. This is a real gap in the ecosystem.

Methodology, limitations, and full data tables are in the article. This is an observational study, not a controlled experiment, and I acknowledge that in the piece. I build Tour Kit (a headless React product tour library), so I have obvious bias toward onboarding tooling. The data and sources are independently verifiable.

Happy to discuss methodology or specific findings.
