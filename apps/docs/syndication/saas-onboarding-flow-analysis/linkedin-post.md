The average SaaS onboarding completion rate is 19.2%.
Top performers hit 70-80%.
That's a 4x gap.

I spent 3 weeks studying 100 SaaS onboarding flows across 12 industries. Here's what the top 10% do differently:

1. They get users to value in under 5 minutes (average is over a day)
2. They ask for 3 signup fields or fewer (each extra field costs 7% conversion)
3. They personalize flows based on role/use case (+40% retention)
4. They use 5-7 item checklists, not 10+ item feature tours
5. They show guidance contextually, not in a linear walkthrough

The most counterintuitive finding: sales-led companies have higher onboarding completion (22.1%) than product-led ones (19%). In a world obsessed with PLG, that's worth sitting with.

The most disappointing finding: fewer than 10 of 100 products had keyboard-navigable onboarding. 15-20% of users have some form of disability. That's a lot of silent churn.
