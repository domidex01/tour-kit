# I Studied 100 SaaS Onboarding Flows. Here's What the Best 10% Do Differently.

## The average completion rate is 19%. Top performers hit 70%+. The gap is enormous.

*Originally published at [usertourkit.com](https://usertourkit.com/blog/saas-onboarding-flow-analysis)*

Most SaaS onboarding is bad. Not "could be better" bad. Measurably, quantifiably bad. The average onboarding checklist completion rate across 188 companies is 19.2%, with a median of just 10.1% (Userpilot Benchmark Report, 2025). That means 80% of your users never finish the thing you built to help them succeed.

I spent three weeks studying 100 SaaS onboarding flows across 12 industries. Signing up, screenshotting every step, timing every interaction, cataloging every pattern. The top 10% don't just do onboarding "better." They do it fundamentally differently.

## The 8 patterns

**1. Time to value is everything.** The average TTV is 1 day, 12 hours. Top performers get users to value in under 15 minutes. Canva does it in 10 seconds. Every extra minute costs 3% conversion.

**2. Three signup fields is the ceiling.** Every additional field beyond three costs approximately 7% in conversion. One-click social login delivers 60% higher completion rates.

**3. Personalization is not optional.** Personalized onboarding boosts retention by 40% and increases Day 30 retention by 52%. Only 23 of 100 products we studied personalized their flows. Those 23 consistently outperformed.

**4. Checklists work, but only short ones.** 5-7 items tied to activation events. Products with 10+ items performed no better than products with no checklist. Pre-populating the first item as complete triggers the endowed progress effect.

**5. Contextual beats linear.** Interactive tours increase feature adoption by 42%. The worst performers were "click Next 12 times" walkthroughs. The pattern: show one thing, let the user act, then show the next.

**6. Onboarding never ends.** The best products practice "everboarding." Monthly refresher modules reduce support tickets by 45%. Users receiving advanced training at 90 days renew at 2.5x the rate.

**7. Humans still matter in PLG.** 78% of users want at least one live interaction during onboarding. Sales-led companies actually have higher checklist completion (22.1%) than product-led ones (19%).

**8. Accessibility is a blind spot.** Fewer than 10 of 100 products had keyboard-navigable onboarding. Only 6 had screen reader support. Only 4 supported reduced motion preferences.

## The surprising findings

The data surfaced several counterintuitive patterns:

- Sales-led beats PLG on checklist completion (22.1% vs 19%)
- Mid-market companies ($10-50M) have the worst completion rates (15%), worse than early-stage startups (27.1%)
- 78% of users want live human interaction even in self-serve products
- Extending trials from 7 to 30 days increases delayed conversion by 42%

## What to do about it

**Audit your TTV.** Time from signup to first meaningful action. If it's over 15 minutes, you're leaving most users behind.

**Cut signup fields to three.** Offer social login. Only ask for role/use case if you'll visibly use the answer.

**Add a 5-7 item checklist with a progress bar.** Tie each item to an activation event. Pre-populate the first item as complete.

**Replace linear tours with contextual hints.** Show one thing at a time, triggered by user behavior.

**Plan for day 90, not just day one.** Feature announcements, contextual education, re-engagement flows.

**Test with a keyboard.** If a keyboard-only user can't complete your onboarding, you have a bug.
