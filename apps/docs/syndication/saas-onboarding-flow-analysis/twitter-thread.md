## Thread (7 tweets)

**1/** I analyzed 100 SaaS onboarding flows across 12 industries.

The average completion rate? 19.2%.
Top performers? 70-80%.

That's a 4x gap. Here are the 8 patterns that cause it:

**2/** Pattern 1: Time to value under 5 minutes.

Average TTV across SaaS: 1 day, 12 hours.
Top 10 products: all under 15 minutes.
Canva: 10 seconds.

Every extra minute costs 3% conversion. The best products don't explain first. They get you doing something real.

**3/** Pattern 2-3: Friction tax + personalization.

Every signup field beyond 3 costs ~7% conversion.
Social login = +60% completion.

But the real unlock: personalization.
Only 23/100 products did it.
Those 23 consistently ranked in the top quartile.
+40% retention. +52% Day 30 retention.

**4/** Pattern 4-5: Short checklists + contextual guidance.

5-7 item checklists: +20-30% completion.
10+ items? Same as no checklist at all.

And linear "click Next 12 times" tours? Worst performers.

The top 10% show one thing, let users act, then show the next.

**5/** The counterintuitive findings:

- Sales-led beats PLG on completion (22.1% vs 19%)
- Mid-market ($10-50M) has WORST rates (15%)
- Early-stage ($1-5M) hits 27.1%
- 78% of users want live human interaction even in self-serve

**6/** The most disappointing finding: accessibility.

- 9/100 had keyboard-navigable onboarding
- 6/100 had screen reader support
- 4/100 respected reduced motion
- ~50/100 failed WCAG color contrast

15-20% of users have some form of disability. That's a lot of silent churn.
