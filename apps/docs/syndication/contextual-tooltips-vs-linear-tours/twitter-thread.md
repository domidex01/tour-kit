## Thread (6 tweets)

**1/** 550 million onboarding interactions analyzed. The result: linear product tours aren't dead, but they're wildly overused. Here's when contextual tooltips beat step-by-step tours (with data).

**2/** Tours exceeding 5 steps lose >50% of users. Self-serve tours (user clicks "show me how") hit 67% completion. Auto-launched tours? About half that. The trigger mechanism matters more than the UI pattern.

**3/** NN/g calls this "push vs pull revelations." Push: auto-launched tours that dump info you don't need yet. Pull: contextual tooltips that surface help when you interact with a feature. Pull wins by 2.5x engagement (58.7% vs 23.7%).

**4/** The sweet spot: linear tours for multi-step setup flows (under 5 steps, user-initiated). Contextual tooltips for feature discovery, power-user paths, and ongoing education. Most products need both.

**5/** What nobody covers: the two patterns have completely different accessibility requirements. Tooltips need aria-describedby + focus triggers. Tours need focus trapping + live region announcements. Getting this wrong locks out 15% of users.
