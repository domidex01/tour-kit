## Thread (6 tweets)

**1/** The average sales rep takes 52 days to log their first deal in a new CRM.

Top performers? 38.

That 14-day gap isn't talent — it's onboarding. I analyzed 6 CRM platforms to find the patterns that close it.

**2/** Pattern 1: Sample data injection.

An empty CRM pipeline is a conversion killer. Users who see an empty pipeline leave in ~8 seconds. Users who see 3 sample deals stay 2+ minutes.

HubSpot and Freshsales do this. You should too.

**3/** Pattern 2: Role-based tour branching.

48% of customers abandon onboarding that doesn't show relevant value quickly (OnRamp survey, n=161).

SDRs, AEs, and RevOps need different first-week experiences. One tour for all roles = 48% gone.

**4/** Pattern 3: Persistent checklists > one-shot tours.

Salesforce's own long product tour "risks overwhelming users" (Appcues teardown).

Zoho's approach works better: persistent checklist + 3-step micro-tours per task. Reps complete tasks across sessions.

**5/** The surprise finding: keyboard shortcuts.

CRM power users live on Ctrl+K, G+D, etc. Product tour overlays that trap focus break all of them.

Nobody in the CRM onboarding space is talking about this failure mode.
