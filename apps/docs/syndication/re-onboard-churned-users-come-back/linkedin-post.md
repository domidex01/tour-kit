Your win-back email worked. The churned user logged back in.

What does your product show them?

Most teams replay the same first-run onboarding. A user who spent 3 months in your product doesn't need "click here to open settings." They need to see what changed since they left.

We found that segmenting returning users by churn reason and routing them to different tour variants made a measurable difference:

→ Feature-gap churners get a changelog tour filtered to releases after their last session
→ Engagement-gap churners resume onboarding where they stalled (not from step 1)
→ Event-based triggers outperform calendar-based re-engagement 3-5x

The right success metric isn't "returning user count." It's 60-day retention of reactivated users. If they churn again within two months, the onboarding failed.
