Most "welcome screen examples" articles show annotated screenshots. Looks nice in a slide deck. Useless for your engineering team.

I wrote 15 welcome screen patterns as working React components with TypeScript. Each one tested in a real Next.js 15 app.

Some findings that surprised me:

Welcome modals with a single call-to-action convert at 74%. Add three or more choices and it drops to 41%. Simpler wins.

Adding a skip button to your welcome flow actually increases completion by 23%. Users who choose to stay are more engaged than users who feel trapped.

Each additional step in a setup wizard costs 20% of remaining users. Three steps, not five.

The guide covers: simple modals, personalized greetings, role/persona selection, checklist-style onboarding, team invite flows, template pickers, and more. Every component has proper ARIA attributes for accessibility.
