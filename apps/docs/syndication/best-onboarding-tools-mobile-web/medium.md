# The Hidden Cost of Cross-Platform Onboarding: What "Mobile Support" Actually Means

## Most tools claim it. Only four deliver native SDKs.

*Originally published at [usertourkit.com](https://usertourkit.com/blog/best-onboarding-tools-mobile-web)*

Most onboarding tools claim "mobile support." What they mean varies wildly. Some ship native iOS and Android SDKs. Others just make their web tooltips responsive. A few let you toggle mobile tours off entirely and call it a feature.

We tested eight tools across web, mobile web, and native mobile. The results: genuine native mobile SDK support starts at $300/month, and only four tools out of eight offer it.

## The price cliff is real

Web-only onboarding tools cost between $0 and $69/month. The moment you need native mobile SDKs, pricing jumps to $299-300/month minimum. Mobile-first platforms start at $999/month. Enterprise tools run $48K+ per year.

That's not a gradual scale. It's a cliff.

## What we found

We evaluated Tour Kit (free, open source, web-only), Appcues ($300/month, broadest SDK coverage), Pendo (free tier at 500 MAU, analytics included), Plotline ($999/month, seven native platforms), Whatfix (enterprise, includes desktop), Userpilot ($299/month, newer mobile support), Chameleon (web-only, strong segmentation), and UserGuiding ($69/month, budget option).

The four tools with genuine native mobile SDKs: Appcues, Pendo, Plotline, and Whatfix. Everyone else is responsive web at best.

## The accessibility gap nobody talks about

WCAG 2.2 now explicitly covers mobile with nine new success criteria, including touch targets and reflow. Not a single tool on our list (besides Tour Kit) publicly documents accessibility compliance for their mobile experiences. For teams building products that need to meet accessibility standards, this is a significant blind spot.

## The real takeaway

Good onboarding drives a 60% increase in conversion rates, according to Smashing Magazine. But 48% of customers abandon onboarding that doesn't show value quickly.

The tool matters less than the flow design. Pick the one that fits your platform, budget, and team, then invest the real effort in what you say to users during their first experience with your product.
