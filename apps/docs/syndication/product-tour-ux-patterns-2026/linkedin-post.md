92% of SaaS apps ship product tours. Only 12% of users rate their onboarding as effective.

That 80-point gap isn't a content problem. It's a pattern selection problem.

I analyzed Chameleon's dataset of 15 million product tour interactions and compiled the UX patterns that actually move completion rates:

Self-serve tours (user opts in) hit 67% completion vs. 53% for auto-triggered. That's a 14-point spread from trigger type alone.

3-step tours hit ~72% completion. Beyond 5 steps, 80% of users bail. The fix: chunked micro-tours scoped to specific features, not one long walkthrough.

Progress bars add +22%. Checklists boost associated tour completion by +21%. Small UX additions with measurable impact.

New in 2026: the European Accessibility Act means WCAG 2.1 AA compliance on tour components is a legal requirement in the EU. Keyboard navigation, focus management, and prefers-reduced-motion are no longer optional.
