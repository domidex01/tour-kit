## Title: The vendor lock-in tax on SaaS onboarding tools — what it actually costs to leave

**Body:**

I've been building Tour Kit (open-source React onboarding library) and spent time researching what happens when teams try to leave platforms like Appcues, Pendo, and Userpilot.

The short version: your tour definitions are proprietary JSON with no portable export. Migration takes 3-4 weeks. Historical analytics data stays with the old vendor. And SaaS pricing is inflating at 8.7% YoY while IT budgets grow at 2.8%.

The number that caught my attention: the real cost of vendor lock-in runs 2-3x the visible platform cost. Pendo averages ~$48K/year with 5-20% renewal uplifts. 60% of SaaS vendors mask price increases through packaging changes.

I'm obviously biased toward the open-source path, but I tried to be honest about when SaaS lock-in is worth accepting — visual builders and mature analytics are real advantages if your team doesn't have frontend engineers.

The article covers the full lock-in playbook, migration cost breakdown, and what "code-owned" onboarding looks like with actual code examples.

https://usertourkit.com/blog/vendor-lock-in-onboarding-tool
