## Title: Vendor lock-in in onboarding tools: what migration actually costs

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/vendor-lock-in-onboarding-tool

## Comment to post immediately after:

I spent a few weeks researching vendor lock-in patterns specific to SaaS onboarding tools (Appcues, Pendo, Userpilot, WalkMe). The playbook is remarkably consistent: proprietary tour definition formats, CSS selector dependencies that break across platforms, analytics data that doesn't export, and annual contracts with auto-renewal.

The data that surprised me most: SaaS price inflation hit 8.7% YoY in 2025 (SaaStr data), while IT budgets grew at 2.8%. And 60% of vendors mask price increases through packaging changes. Migration between platforms takes 3-4 weeks according to Product Fruits, with historical analytics data lost entirely.

I built an open-source alternative (Tour Kit), so my bias is obvious. I tried to be honest about when SaaS lock-in is worth accepting — visual builders and mature analytics pipelines are genuine advantages for teams without frontend engineers. The article covers the full cost breakdown and what "code-owned onboarding" looks like in practice.
