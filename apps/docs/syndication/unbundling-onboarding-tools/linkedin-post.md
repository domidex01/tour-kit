$285 billion in SaaS market cap evaporated in early 2026. The unbundling has started.

Onboarding tools are next in line. The all-in-one platforms that promised to handle everything (tours, checklists, analytics, surveys) are losing ground to modular stacks where teams pick the best tool for each layer.

Why now? Three forces:

Performance is measurable. WalkMe injects 180-350KB. Pendo adds 150-250KB. Core Web Vitals made that visible in search rankings.

Lock-in is expensive. Teams estimate 3-6 months to migrate off Pendo. Multi-year contracts with hidden auto-renewals make it worse.

Teams already own 60-70% of the stack. Analytics, feature flags, component libraries, state management. The only missing piece is onboarding-specific logic.

Deloitte found 46% of IT teams have implemented composable architecture, reporting 37% shorter time-to-market and 30% higher ROI. Gartner projected 60% of organizations would list "composability" as a strategic goal by 2026.
