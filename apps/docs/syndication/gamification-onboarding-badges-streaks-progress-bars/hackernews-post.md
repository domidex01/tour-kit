## Title: A meta-analysis found progress bars can decrease onboarding completion

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/gamification-onboarding-badges-streaks-progress-bars

## Comment to post immediately after:

I wrote this after finding a counterintuitive result in behavioral economics research: Irrational Labs analyzed 32 experiments and found that progress bars hurt completion when the bar decelerates (fast start, slow finish). Bars that accelerate toward the end perform significantly better.

The other finding that surprised me was about placement. Progress bars at the top of a page increased drop-off. At the bottom, they improved completion. The explanation: top placement feels like a reminder of work remaining, bottom placement feels like a reward for progress already made.

The article covers three gamification mechanics (progress bars, streaks, badges) with the psychology behind each, benchmark data from Userpilot and UserGuiding, and accessible React implementations. Duolingo's streak system is the most studied example: 7-day streak users are 3.6x more likely to stay active, and their Streak Freeze reduced churn by 21%.

I also included a section on when gamification actively hurts. Developer tools, enterprise B2B with mandatory adoption, and irregularly-used products are the three categories where I've seen it backfire consistently.
