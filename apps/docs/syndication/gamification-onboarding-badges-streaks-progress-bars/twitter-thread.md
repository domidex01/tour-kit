## Thread (7 tweets)

**1/** The median SaaS onboarding checklist completion rate is 10.1%.

That means ~80% of your new users quit before reaching the features they signed up for.

I researched what actually fixes this. Some findings were counterintuitive.

**2/** Progress bars increase completion by ~40%. But a meta-analysis of 32 experiments found they DECREASE completion when early progress feels slow.

The fix: front-load difficult steps so the bar accelerates toward the end. Users who feel momentum building finish more often.

**3/** The "endowed progress effect" is real. Pre-fill your progress bar to 10-20% before users do anything.

Credit them for creating an account, verifying email, landing on the page. This has been replicated consistently since 2006.

**4/** Duolingo's streak system is the most studied case:
- 7-day streak users: 3.6x more likely to stay active
- iOS widget showing streaks: +60% daily commitment
- Streak Freeze alone: -21% churn among at-risk users

Without freeze mechanics, one missed day kills the streak AND the user's motivation.

**5/** Badges work when they validate real progress. "Logged in 5 times" = patronizing. "Built your first dashboard" = genuine competence.

Apps with meaningful badges see 50% higher completion rates.

**6/** When gamification HURTS:
- Developer tools (engineers find badges condescending)
- Enterprise B2B mandatory rollouts (streaks create anxiety)
- Irregular-use products (daily mechanics punish weekends)

Match the mechanic to your product's natural usage pattern.
