I spent three weeks building a SaaS pricing page for my React library before I deleted it.

Here's what changed my mind:

Tour Kit is a product tour library. It ships in your bundle, runs locally, and has no server infrastructure. The marginal cost of one more user is approximately zero.

So why was I copying the pricing model of tools that run cloud infrastructure at $249/month?

The data reinforced the instinct: 78% of finance decision-makers report increased scrutiny on recurring software costs. 62% of businesses are actively consolidating their SaaS portfolios. Subscription fatigue isn't just a consumer problem anymore.

I landed on $99 one-time. Three core packages stay MIT-licensed and free. Eight Pro packages cost $99 once. No seats, no MAU limits, no renewal emails.

The honest tradeoff: no MRR means I'm starting from zero every month. But in a market where the average mid-size company manages 130+ SaaS subscriptions, "pay once, use forever" is its own kind of differentiation.
