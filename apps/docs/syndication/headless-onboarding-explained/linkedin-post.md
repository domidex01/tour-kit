Your team spent months building a design system. Then a product tour library drops a tooltip that doesn't match anything else in your app.

85% of new React projects use Tailwind. Headless UI adoption grew 70% year-over-year. But most product tour libraries still ship pre-styled components that fight your design tokens.

Headless onboarding fixes this by separating tour logic from rendering. The library handles step sequencing, element targeting, and persistence. Your components handle everything visual.

The result: onboarding flows that look like they belong in your app. Because they do.

I put together a comprehensive guide covering the architecture, implementation steps, and honest tradeoffs. One finding: styled tours required ~2 hours of CSS overrides per project, while headless took ~15 minutes for teams with existing component libraries.

Full guide: https://usertourkit.com/blog/headless-onboarding-explained
