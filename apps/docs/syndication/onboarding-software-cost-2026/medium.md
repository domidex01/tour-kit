# How Much Does Onboarding Software Really Cost?

## A 3-year TCO comparison of SaaS tools, in-house builds, and open-source libraries

*Originally published at [usertourkit.com](https://usertourkit.com/blog/onboarding-software-cost-2026)*

Every pricing page tells the same story: a monthly number, a feature grid, a "Contact Sales" button for anything above mid-market. None of them tell you what you'll actually spend over three years once MAU overages, implementation fees, and maintenance hours are factored in.

We calculated total cost of ownership across three approaches — SaaS tools, building from scratch, and open-source headless libraries — using real salary data, published pricing, and first-hand build estimates from engineering teams who've done all three.

## The range is staggering

Onboarding software costs between $0 and $142,000 per year in 2026. The total cost over three years ranges from under $1,000 for a developer-owned headless library to over $500,000 for an enterprise digital adoption platform. The median SaaS entry price sits at $279–$300 per month for 1,000–2,000 monthly active users.

## SaaS pricing (what vendors don't highlight)

The sticker price is the beginning. Here's what major vendors actually charge:

- **Userpilot:** $299/mo for 2,000 MAUs
- **Appcues:** $300/mo for 1,000 MAUs; Growth plan is $750/mo
- **Chameleon:** $279/mo
- **UserGuiding:** $69/mo
- **Pendo:** Free tier, then $15,000–$142,000/year
- **WalkMe:** $9,000–$50,000/year

The catch? All of these scale with monthly active users. A product growing from 2,000 to 20,000 MAUs can see its onboarding bill increase 5–10x. Add implementation fees ($2,000–$50,000), staff training (20–30 hours per admin), and compliance upsells — the real number is 2–5x the sticker price.

## Building from scratch costs more than you think

Appcues published a cost breakdown based on real team compositions. A minimum viable onboarding system requires roughly:

- 1 UX designer, 1 product manager, 3 engineers over a 2-month sprint
- **Year 1 total: approximately $70,784**
- **Annual maintenance: $25,766/year**

At enterprise scale, Atlassian's growth team spent $3 million over three years on custom onboarding.

## The option nobody talks about

Every "build vs. buy" article presents two choices. But there's a third: headless libraries that handle the hard parts (positioning, accessibility, state management) while you keep full design and data control.

The 3-year TCO comparison:

- **SaaS tool (Appcues):** $15,400–$39,800
- **Build from scratch:** $122,316
- **Headless library:** ~$8,099

That headless number includes $99 for the library license and developer time for integration and maintenance. No MAU fees. No vendor lock-in. MIT license.

## When each approach makes sense

**Choose SaaS** when your team is non-technical, you need it running within a week, and your MAU count is stable.

**Build from scratch** when you have unique interaction patterns and a dedicated growth engineering team.

**Choose a headless library** when your team already uses React, you have an existing design system, and you want full data ownership.

The right answer depends on your team. But the right *question* is always about 3-year TCO, not monthly sticker price.
