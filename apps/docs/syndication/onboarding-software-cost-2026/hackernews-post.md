## Title: The real cost of onboarding software: 3-year TCO from $8K to $500K

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/onboarding-software-cost-2026

## Comment to post immediately after:

I put together a cost analysis of onboarding software (product tours, tooltips, onboarding flows) across three approaches: SaaS tools, building from scratch, and headless open-source libraries.

The data sources are published vendor pricing pages, Appcues' own build-cost research ($70K Year 1 for a startup team), and Userpilot's documentation of Atlassian spending $3M over 3 years on custom onboarding.

The most interesting finding: every "build vs buy" article I found presents a false binary. A headless library that handles positioning, accessibility, and state management eliminates 70-80% of custom engineering work. The 3-year TCO comes to ~$8K vs $15K-$40K for SaaS vs $122K for building from scratch.

The MAU pricing model is the biggest hidden cost in SaaS tools. A product growing from 2K to 20K users can see a 5-10x price increase with no feature change. None of the vendor pricing pages make this obvious.

Disclosure: I built Tour Kit, one of the libraries mentioned. The cost comparisons use the vendors' own published data.
