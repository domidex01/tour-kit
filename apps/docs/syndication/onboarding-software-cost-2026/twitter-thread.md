**1/** Onboarding software costs between $0 and $142,000/year. I calculated the 3-year TCO for SaaS tools, building from scratch, and headless libraries. The numbers are wild.

**2/** SaaS tools (Appcues, Userpilot, Pendo) cost $15K-$40K over 3 years. The gotcha: MAU-based pricing. Growing from 2K to 20K users can push your bill from $3,600/yr to $24,000/yr. Same features, just more users.

**3/** Building from scratch costs $122K over 3 years. Appcues' own research: $70K Year 1 (3 engineers + PM + designer, 2-month sprint), then $25K/yr maintenance. Atlassian spent $3M over 3 years.

**4/** The option nobody models: headless libraries. ~$8K over 3 years. The library handles positioning, a11y, and state. You render your own components. No MAU fees. No vendor lock-in.

**5/** 5 cost mistakes I see repeatedly: ignoring MAU projections, comparing sticker prices, trusting "free tiers," treating open source as zero-cost, and forgetting the performance hit from third-party JS injection.
