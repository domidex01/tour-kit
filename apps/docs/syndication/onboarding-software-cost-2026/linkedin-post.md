How much does onboarding software actually cost over 3 years?

Not the pricing page number. The real total — MAU overages, implementation fees, maintenance hours, vendor lock-in.

I ran the numbers across three approaches:

SaaS tools (Appcues, Userpilot, Pendo): $15,400-$39,800 over 3 years. The hidden cost is MAU-based pricing — growing from 2K to 20K users can increase your bill 5-10x with no feature change.

Building from scratch: $122,316 over 3 years. Atlassian's growth team spent $3M. Even a startup-scale build runs $70K in year one.

Headless open-source libraries: ~$8,099 over 3 years. The library handles the hard engineering (positioning, accessibility, state management). You keep full code and data ownership. No recurring fees.

The "build vs buy" framing that most articles use is missing an option. For teams with an existing React codebase and design system, a headless library eliminates 70-80% of custom engineering at a fraction of the SaaS cost.
