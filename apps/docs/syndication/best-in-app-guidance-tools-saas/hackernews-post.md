## Title: In-App Guidance Tools for SaaS: Comparing 10 Options from $69/mo to $405K/yr

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/best-in-app-guidance-tools-saas

## Comment to post immediately after:

I tested 10 in-app guidance tools for SaaS across three categories: headless open-source libraries, mid-market no-code platforms, and enterprise digital adoption platforms.

The pricing range is striking. UserGuiding starts at $69/month. WalkMe enterprise contracts run $79,000-$405,000/year. Mid-market platforms (Userpilot, Appcues, Userflow) cluster around $240-$300/month. Open-source options (Shepherd.js at 13K+ GitHub stars, React Joyride at 7.6K) are free under AGPL/MIT.

The most interesting finding: no major vendor publicly benchmarks their bundle size impact on client performance. SaaS platform overlay scripts add 80-200KB of JavaScript. For teams that care about Core Web Vitals, that's a meaningful trade-off against the convenience of no-code builders.

AI is the dominant 2026 trend — Userflow's Smartflow generates flows from plain language, CommandBar uses an AI search bar instead of traditional tours, and Pendo's guide builder suggests steps from behavioral data.

Disclosure: I built Tour Kit, one of the headless options listed. I've tried to be fair in the comparison and every data point is sourced from public docs, G2, or npm.
