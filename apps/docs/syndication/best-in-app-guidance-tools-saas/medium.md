# 10 In-App Guidance Tools for SaaS, Ranked by a Developer Who Tested Them

## The market spans $69/month to $405,000/year. Here's how to navigate it.

*Originally published at [usertourkit.com](https://usertourkit.com/blog/best-in-app-guidance-tools-saas)*

The in-app guidance market spans from $69/month self-serve platforms to $405,000/year enterprise contracts. Gartner predicted 70% of organizations would adopt digital adoption platforms by 2025, and that milestone has arrived. But "in-app guidance" now means wildly different things depending on who's selling it.

We tested ten tools across both categories — headless code libraries and no-code SaaS platforms. Here's what we found.

## How we evaluated

We scored each tool on six criteria: ease of setup, feature depth, pricing transparency, analytics, accessibility, and performance impact. For code-based tools, we installed each in a Vite 6 + React 19 project and built a five-step onboarding tour. For no-code platforms, we used their Chrome extension builders.

Full disclosure: Tour Kit is our project. We've tried to be fair, but you should know that upfront.

## The 10 tools

**1. Tour Kit** — Headless React library. Under 8KB gzipped, WCAG 2.1 AA compliant, works with any design system. Free MIT core, $99 one-time for pro packages. Best for React teams wanting code ownership.

**2. Pendo** — The only platform combining analytics, guidance, session replay, and feedback. Free tier for 500 MAUs, enterprise pricing around $48K/year median. Best for analytics-driven enterprise teams.

**3. Userpilot** — PLG platform with built-in A/B testing and retroactive analytics. Smoobu reported a 17% conversion boost. Starts at $299/month. Best for data-driven onboarding.

**4. Appcues** — Widest selection of UI patterns among no-code platforms. Fastest time-to-first-tour in our testing. $300/month starting. Best for fast no-code deployment.

**5. Chameleon** — Most visual control among no-code tools. Embeddable cards, granular CSS, developer-friendly APIs. Free tier available. Best for design-focused teams.

**6. Userflow** — AI assistant (Smartflow) generates flows from plain language. Canvas-style builder, strong localization. $240/month. Best for AI-assisted onboarding.

**7. CommandBar** — Universal AI search bar (Spotlight for your app) instead of traditional tour overlays. Adapts to user behavior in real time. Best for developer-focused SaaS.

**8. WalkMe** — Enterprise heavyweight for multi-app workflows (SAP, Salesforce, Workday). $79K-$405K/year. Best for large-scale digital transformation.

**9. UserGuiding** — Most affordable at ~$69/month. Covers walkthroughs, checklists, NPS. Best for budget-conscious startups.

**10. Shepherd.js** — Top open-source option with 13,000+ GitHub stars. Framework-agnostic. AGPL license (commercial: $50-$300). Best for non-React teams.

## How to choose

If your team has frontend developers and you want full control: pick a headless library (Tour Kit or Shepherd.js).

If your product team needs to ship flows without engineering: pick a mid-market platform (Userpilot, Appcues, Chameleon). Budget $240-$300/month.

If you need multi-app guidance at enterprise scale: pick Pendo or WalkMe. Budget $50K+/year.

If you're pre-Series A: UserGuiding at $69/month covers the basics.

Bundle size matters on mobile. Tour Kit's core is under 8KB gzipped. Shepherd.js is 37KB. SaaS platform scripts add 80-200KB.
