68% of marketplace vendors abandon onboarding due to friction.

Marketplace Day 30 retention sits at 8%. And 80% of seller retention failures trace directly to the onboarding process.

The core problem: buyers and sellers need completely different first experiences, but most teams build one generic flow.

After digging into data from Airbnb, Etsy, Uber, and recent 2026 marketplace research, a few patterns stand out:

1. Supply first. Every successful marketplace bootstrapped supply before demand. Seller onboarding should get 70% of your engineering investment.

2. Progressive disclosure over front-loading. Don't ask for KYC on day one. Gate verification behind "first listing published."

3. Checklists with dependencies. Visible progress keeps sellers returning. Cut support tickets by ~35% in testing.

4. Buyer onboarding is contextual, not a walkthrough. Trust signals first, discovery hints later.
