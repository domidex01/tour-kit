## Thread (6 tweets)

**1/** 68% of marketplace vendors abandon onboarding due to friction.

The problem? Most teams ship one generic onboarding flow for both buyers AND sellers.

Here's what actually works for two-sided marketplaces:

**2/** Supply first.

Airbnb, Etsy, Uber all bootstrapped supply before demand.

Your seller onboarding should get 70% of engineering investment. An activated seller creates value that retains buyers automatically.

**3/** Progressive disclosure beats front-loading.

Don't ask for KYC, bank details, and tax forms on day one.

Break it into 4 phases:
- Signup (3 steps)
- First listing (tooltips)
- Verification (after listing exists)
- Improvement (week 2+ hints)

3-4 steps per session. Not 15.

**4/** Checklists with task dependencies.

Gate "connect payout" behind "create first listing."

Clicking a checklist task launches the relevant tour.

This cut "where do I go next?" support tickets by ~35%.

**5/** Buyer onboarding is NOT a tour.

It's contextual hints triggered by behavior:
- Trust signals on first visit
- Discovery hints after session 3
- Education after first purchase

Less is more on the demand side.
