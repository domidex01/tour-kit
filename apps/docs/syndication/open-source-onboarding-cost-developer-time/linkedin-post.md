"Free" is the most expensive word in software.

I calculated the total cost of ownership for open source product tour libraries, and the numbers were eye-opening. At typical developer rates, a "free" onboarding library costs $10,000-$20,000 in year one when you add integration, monthly maintenance, and the feature add-ons your product team will inevitably request.

Two data points that stood out:
- The 2024 Tidelift report found 60% of OSS maintainers work unpaid and 44% cite burnout
- Enterprises underestimate software costs by 2x-4x (Software Pricing Guide)

The counterargument matters too: React Joyride is genuinely great for MVPs. Not every project needs analytics, checklists, and user segmentation from day one. The cost argument only applies when requirements grow.
