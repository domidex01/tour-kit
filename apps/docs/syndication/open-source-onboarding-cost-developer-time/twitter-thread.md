**1/** Your "free" product tour library probably costs $12,000/year in developer time.

I did the math. Here's what nobody tells you about the real cost of open source onboarding tools:

**2/** The 2024 Tidelift report found 60% of OSS maintainers work unpaid. 44% cite burnout. 60% have quit or considered quitting.

When your tour library's maintainer disappears, migration costs 40-80 hours ($4,000-$12,000).

**3/** The hidden costs:

- Integration: 40-80 hrs ($2,800-$6,000)
- Selector maintenance: 5-10 hrs/month
- React version upgrades: weeks when your library lags
- Feature creep: analytics month 3, checklists month 6

Year 1 total at $100/hr: $10,000-$20,000

**4/** For comparison:

- SaaS (Appcues/Userpilot): $4,400-$11,400/yr (but you don't own the code)
- Code-owned library: $1,100-$2,200/yr (you own everything)

The "free" option is the most expensive in year one.

**5/** When IS free the right call?

- MVPs where speed > long-term cost
- Simple tours (under 10 steps, no analytics)
- Teams with deep React expertise

React Joyride has 600K+ weekly downloads for good reason. Not every project needs the full stack.
