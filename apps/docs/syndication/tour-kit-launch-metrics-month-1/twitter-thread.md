**1/** I shipped an open source React library 30 days ago.

**2/** The metrics dashboard:

- Day 1: 12 stars, 8 downloads, 31 docs visitors
- Day 7: 47 stars, 34 downloads, 112 visitors
- Day 30: 183 stars, 89 downloads, 487 visitors

Not viral. Not zero either.

**3/** What worked: content-first launch.

34 blog articles in month 1. Comparisons and architecture posts drove more traffic than tutorials.

73% of developers want tutorials first (202-dev survey). But comparisons are what they search for.

**4/** What flopped: posting to Reddit r/reactjs on a Friday evening.

4 upvotes. Zero comments. Gone.

Tanner Linsley (TanStack): "The existence of a great product is not enough for it to be a success."

Distribution > code quality at launch.

**5/** The metrics that actually matter aren't stars or downloads:

- First issue from a stranger: day 4
- First external PR: day 19
- First unprompted mention: day 22
- First "I switched from X": not yet

That last one is the real conversion signal.

**6/** What I'd cut from the launch:

- The Pro pricing page (too early, adds friction)
- 7 of 10 packages (ship 3, add based on demand)
- Platform-specific announcement templates (write one genuine post instead)
