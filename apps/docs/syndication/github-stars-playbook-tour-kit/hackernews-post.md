## Comment to post immediately after:

I built Tour Kit, a headless product tour library for React, as a solo developer starting in late 2025. This post documents every growth tactic I tried to get from 0 to 1,000 GitHub stars without paid advertising.

Some findings that surprised me:

- A Carnegie Mellon study found ~6 million suspected fake stars on GitHub (15.8% of repos with 50+ stars involved in campaigns). This makes authentic growth harder but also more valuable.

- My first "Show HN" post got 4 upvotes and died. Research on 138 HN launches shows the average repo gains 121 stars in 24h from the front page, but "Show HN" as a tag has no statistical advantage after controlling for post quality.

- Reddit r/reactjs outperformed every other channel for actual npm installs (not just stars). One indie dev documented 143K views from a single subreddit post.

- The biggest sustained growth driver was SEO content targeting keywords that competing libraries (React Joyride, Shepherd.js, Driver.js) completely ignored because none of them publish blog content.

- The 2026-specific insight: shipping llms.txt files so AI coding assistants can discover your library is a genuine new growth channel that most projects haven't adopted yet.

The article includes specific numbers, research citations, and the full subreddit conversion ranking. I'm being honest about what didn't work (Product Hunt, Twitter, awesome lists too early) because those failures are as instructive as the wins.

Tour Kit itself: 10 composable packages, <12KB gzipped, MIT core, works with shadcn/ui and Tailwind.
