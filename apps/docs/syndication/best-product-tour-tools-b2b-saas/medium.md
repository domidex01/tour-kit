# The Real Cost of Enterprise Product Tour Tools in 2026

## From free open-source to $405K/year contracts — what B2B SaaS teams actually need

*Originally published at [usertourkit.com](https://usertourkit.com/blog/best-product-tour-tools-b2b-saas)*

Enterprise product tour tools range from $0 open-source libraries to $405,000/year platform contracts. Most enterprise buyers sign six-figure deals without ever comparing the technical tradeoffs. That's expensive guesswork.

We tested eight tools on SSO support, analytics depth, compliance certifications, pricing transparency, and developer experience. Here's what we found.

### The numbers that matter

- 67% of SaaS companies use product tours to boost activation rates by 30-50%
- Four-step tours hit 60.1% completion rates. Five+ steps drops below 20%
- WalkMe averages $78K/year per contract. Pendo ranges $15K-$142K/year
- Mid-market tools (Userpilot, Appcues, Chameleon) run $3K-$10K/year
- Open-source options like Tour Kit cost $0 for the MIT core

### The three tiers

**Headless libraries** — Tour Kit ships under 8KB gzipped, gives your React team full design control, and costs $99 one-time for Pro features. The catch: no visual builder, your developers build the tours in code.

**Mid-market platforms** — Userpilot ($249/month), Appcues ($299/month), and Chameleon ($279/month) give product managers no-code builders. SSO and audit logs cost extra on enterprise tiers.

**Enterprise DAPs** — Pendo, WalkMe, and Whatfix target Fortune 500 deployments across multiple applications. Budget $15K-$100K+/year plus implementation fees.

### The accessibility blind spot

None of these platforms prominently document WCAG 2.1 AA compliance for their tour overlays. If your B2B SaaS serves healthcare, government, or financial services, that's a procurement risk worth investigating.

### How to choose

The question isn't "which tool has more features." It's "who builds the tours at your company?"

If it's engineers: headless library. If it's product managers: mid-market platform. If it's L&D teams at a 10,000-person company: enterprise DAP.

The one metric that matters most in 2026? Time-to-value. Pick the tool that gets users to their first "aha moment" fastest.
