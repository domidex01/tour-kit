## Title: In-app NPS vs post-tour feedback: matching survey types to onboarding moments

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/in-app-nps-vs-post-tour-feedback

## Comment to post immediately after:

I wrote this after noticing a pattern in SaaS onboarding: most product tours trigger NPS right at tour completion, which is the wrong survey at the wrong moment.

NPS measures recommendation intent (loyalty), which needs weeks of product experience to form. A user who just finished onboarding 30 seconds ago scores based on tour quality, not product loyalty. We call this the honeymoon effect — inflated NPS scores that don't predict retention.

The fix is mapping survey types to lifecycle moments: CES (Customer Effort Score) at specific tour steps to measure friction, CSAT at tour completion to rate the experience, and NPS deferred 14-30 days to when users have formed real opinions.

Some numbers: in-app surveys hit 20-40% response rates vs 5-15% for email. Adding open-text follow-up to a scale question increases abandonment 24%. 49% of companies already combine NPS with at least one other metric.

The article includes React code examples for event-driven survey triggers with lifecycle-aware fatigue prevention. Also covers the accessibility gap — most survey widgets fail WCAG 2.1 AA, which means your feedback data has a blind spot covering everyone who uses a screen reader.
