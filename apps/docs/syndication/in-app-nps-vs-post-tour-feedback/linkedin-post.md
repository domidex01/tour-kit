Most product tours trigger an NPS survey the moment a user finishes onboarding. That's the wrong survey at the wrong moment.

NPS measures loyalty. A user who completed a tour 12 seconds ago hasn't formed a loyalty opinion. They score 8 or 9 because the tour was nice, then churn 60 days later. We call this the honeymoon effect.

The fix is matching survey types to lifecycle moments:
- CES at specific tour steps (measures friction while it's fresh)
- CSAT at tour completion (rates the experience itself)
- NPS 14-30 days later (when users have formed real opinions)

Some numbers that shaped our approach: in-app surveys hit 20-40% response rates vs 5-15% for email. Adding open-text follow-up increases abandonment 24%. And 49% of companies already combine NPS with at least one other metric.

The other thing nobody discusses: survey fatigue during onboarding. Users are already learning new workflows. Stacking a survey on top increases abandonment of the flow itself.
