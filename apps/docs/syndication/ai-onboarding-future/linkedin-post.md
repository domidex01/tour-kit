Every onboarding tool vendor shipped an AI feature in 2025. AI-generated tour copy. "Smart segmentation." AI-driven step recommendations.

Here's what I think actually changes about product onboarding — and what doesn't — from the perspective of someone who writes onboarding infrastructure, not someone who sells it.

What changes: AI will personalize step sequencing, timing, and content. Userpilot's beta suggests 15-20% better completion rates with model-predicted timing vs fixed delays. That's real.

What doesn't change: accessibility, user control, performance budgets, and the component layer. WCAG 2.1 AA compliance requires deterministic solutions. Focus management doesn't get solved by a model. And loading 200KB of AI inference to show a tooltip is a bad trade on mobile.

My take: invest in the layer that's hardest to change later (the component layer). AI models improve every quarter whether you do anything or not. But a bad rendering foundation means rebuilding everything when you want to add AI later.
