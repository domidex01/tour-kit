Your AI product's biggest UX problem isn't the AI. It's the blank text box.

Traditional SaaS products have buttons and menus that tell users what to do. AI products present an empty prompt input and hope users figure it out. The average activation rate across B2B SaaS is 37.5%. For AI products without prompt guidance, it's lower.

I studied 150+ AI products and found three patterns that consistently improve activation:

1. Template activation loops (show a working prompt, let users remix it, then bridge to freeform)
2. The 60-seconds-to-value framework (users need meaningful AI output within a minute)
3. Guided prompt tours that teach prompting concepts, not just where to click

The counterintuitive finding: when onboarding automates too much, users never learn to prompt. Faster first success, but shallower skill development.
