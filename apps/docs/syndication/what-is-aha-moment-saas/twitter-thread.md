## Thread (6 tweets)

**1/** Every SaaS product has a moment where users stop evaluating and start believing.

Sean Ellis called it the "aha moment" — when the utility of the product really clicks.

I mapped documented aha moments from 6 well-known products. Here's the pattern:

**2/** Slack: ~2,000 messages exchanged within a team
Canva: creating a first design with a template
Dropbox: saving a file on one device, seeing it on another
Zoom: two stages — joining a call, then hosting one
Twilio: sending a first API-triggered message

**3/** Notice: none require understanding every feature.

Every aha moment is tied to ONE narrow action that proves value. The moment is narrow by design.

**4/** The data is stark:

- Products delivering the aha moment in session 1 see 2.4x higher trial-to-paid conversion
- Top PLG companies hit it in under 5 minutes (best: 2-3 min)
- 3-step product tours: 72% completion
- 7-step tours: 16%

**5/** How to find yours:

1. Compare retained users vs churned — first-session behavior
2. Identify 2-3 actions that correlate with 30-day retention
3. Map your onboarding to surface those actions ASAP

Tools: Amplitude, Mixpanel, or PostHog cohort analysis.
