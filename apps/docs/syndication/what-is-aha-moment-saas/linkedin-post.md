Every SaaS product has a moment where users stop evaluating and start believing.

Sean Ellis called it the "aha moment" — when the utility of the product really clicks for the user. Not onboarding completion. Not feature comprehension. The emotional realization that this product matters.

I mapped documented aha moments from six products:

→ Slack: ~2,000 team messages
→ Dropbox: file appears on second device
→ Zoom: two stages (join a call, then host one)
→ Twilio: first API-triggered message sent

The data: products delivering the aha moment within the first session see 2.4x higher trial-to-paid conversion. Top PLG companies hit it in under 5 minutes.

The takeaway for product teams: your onboarding flow should be engineered around one narrow action that proves value. Not a feature tour. Not a settings walkthrough.
