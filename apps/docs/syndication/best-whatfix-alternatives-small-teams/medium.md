# Your team doesn't need a $24K/year onboarding tool

## 7 practical alternatives to Whatfix for small teams that ship fast and cost less

*Originally published at [usertourkit.com](https://usertourkit.com/blog/best-whatfix-alternatives-small-teams)*

Whatfix starts at roughly $24,000 per year. The average customer pays closer to $32,000. Implementation takes one to three months before your first tooltip goes live.

For a 10-person startup that just needs onboarding flows, that math doesn't work. You need tools that ship in days, not months.

We evaluated seven alternatives across budget fit, setup speed, technical overhead, and feature scope. Here's what we found.

**The cheapest SaaS option:** Product Fruits at $79/month for 1,500 MAU ($0.053 per user). It bundles tours, checklists, surveys, and a knowledge base into one dashboard with no sales calls required.

**The best value per user:** Userflow at $240/month gives you 3,000 MAU ($0.080/MAU) with the highest G2 rating in the category at 4.8 out of 5.

**The budget pick:** Usetiful at $29/month uses assist-based pricing instead of per-MAU, making costs predictable for low-traffic apps.

**The developer option:** Tour Kit is a headless React library shipping at under 8KB gzipped. Free tier under MIT, and $99 one-time for Pro packages. No per-user charges. No recurring fees. The catch: you need React developers to build and maintain tours.

**The open-source option:** Intro.js has been around since 2013. Tiny footprint at 10KB gzipped, works with any framework. But it uses direct DOM manipulation, which conflicts with React's virtual DOM.

**The startup pick:** UserGuiding at $174/month for 2,000 MAU offers NPS surveys on all plans and costs less than Appcues or Chameleon.

**The segmentation play:** Chameleon at $279/month gives solo product managers strong targeting based on user properties and events, backed by benchmark data from 550M+ data points.

The common thread: Whatfix is built for enterprises with dedicated adoption teams and $30K+ budgets. If that's not you, every tool on this list is a better fit.

Disclosure: We built Tour Kit, so take our analysis with appropriate skepticism. Full comparison table with pricing, G2 ratings, and honest tradeoffs for each tool in the [complete article](https://usertourkit.com/blog/best-whatfix-alternatives-small-teams).
