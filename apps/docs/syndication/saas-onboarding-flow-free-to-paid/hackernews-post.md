## Title: 7 SaaS Onboarding Flows That Convert Free to Paid (Slack, Notion, Figma, Dropbox, Canva, Loom, Ahrefs)

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/saas-onboarding-flow-free-to-paid

## Comment to post immediately after:

I studied the onboarding flows from seven SaaS products to understand why some convert free users at 2-3x the industry average.

The core finding: products that convert well don't give feature tours. They identify 2-3 activation actions (the behaviors most correlated with retention) and optimize the first session around reaching those actions. Slack's is sending 2,000 messages. Canva's is exporting a design. Dropbox's is installing the desktop app.

The most interesting pattern was the "reverse trial" used by Ahrefs, Toggl, and Grammarly. Instead of starting free and upselling, they give full paid access for 7 days and then drop users to a limited tier. Loss aversion makes users feel the downgrade viscerally, and it converts at 2-3x traditional free tiers according to Lenny Rachitsky's research.

The article includes React/TypeScript code examples for implementing each pattern, but the patterns themselves are framework-agnostic. Disclosure: I build Tour Kit, an open-source product tour library for React, so some examples use it. The analysis of the seven products is independent of that.
