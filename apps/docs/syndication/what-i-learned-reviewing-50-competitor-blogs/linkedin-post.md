I read 50 SaaS onboarding tool blogs in two weeks. Every single one from Appcues, Userpilot, Pendo, WalkMe, Whatfix, and a dozen others.

What I found: every vendor runs the same playbook. Self-serving "honest" comparisons. Listicles where they rank #1. Feature guides that funnel into demos. 200+ "X vs Y" posts across the top 10 vendors alone.

The biggest blind spot? Nobody writes for developers. Every blog targets PMs and marketers. But somebody has to build the tour. That person is a developer, and they're completely ignored.

Two data points stood out:
- 31% of content marketers now budget $15K-$45K/month on content (up from 19% in 2025)
- AI drafting usage dropped from 57% to 44% this year -- the market is already self-correcting

The takeaway for anyone building content for a developer tool: write for the implementer, not the buyer. Publish data competitors won't share. And stop pretending your comparison articles are objective.
