## Title: What I learned from reading 50 SaaS onboarding tool blogs

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/what-i-learned-reviewing-50-competitor-blogs

## Comment to post immediately after:

I spent two weeks reading every blog post from the major SaaS onboarding and product tour vendors (Appcues, Userpilot, Pendo, WalkMe, Whatfix, and about 15 others). I'm building a headless product tour library for React and wanted to understand the content space before publishing anything.

Key findings: every vendor runs an identical content playbook (self-serving comparisons, listicles where they rank #1, feature guides that funnel to demos). The top 10 vendors have 200+ "X vs Y" posts between them. Nobody writes for developers -- every blog targets PMs and marketers, despite developers being the ones who implement the tours. And AI content is everywhere but already declining -- AI drafting usage dropped from 57% to 44% between 2025 and 2026 (CMI/Backlinko data).

The most underused format: Samuel Hulick's UserOnboard-style teardowns. He built a huge audience with 60+ screenshot teardowns of onboarding flows. Every vendor references his work, but none do teardowns themselves.

Biggest blind spot: nobody publishes first-party performance data (bundle sizes, Lighthouse scores, Core Web Vitals impact). Happy to discuss methodology or specific patterns I found.
