Only 12% of US workers rate their company's onboarding experience favorably. Internal tools get the worst of it.

Most engineering teams build sophisticated internal dashboards, CRMs, and admin panels — then train employees with a Confluence page and a 45-minute screen share. New hires forget 80% by lunch.

The fix is in-app guidance: product tours embedded directly in the interface that trigger based on role and disappear once completed. Sales sees the pipeline walkthrough. Finance sees reporting. Support sees the ticket queue.

I wrote a guide covering the patterns that work: role-based tours, first-time user detection, version-keyed change tours for post-deploy updates, and WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility (which is a legal requirement for many orgs under ADA/Section 508).

The interesting tradeoff: digital adoption platforms like WalkMe solve this but cost $10K-100K+/yr. A code-owned React library costs a fraction and lives in your repo.
