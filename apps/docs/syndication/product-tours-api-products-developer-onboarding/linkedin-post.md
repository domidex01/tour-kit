82% of organizations are API-first. 74% generate at least 10% of revenue from APIs.

But most API companies invest in documentation and ignore the developer dashboard experience entirely.

I researched the onboarding flows of Stripe, Twilio, Postman, Google Cloud, and Vonage. The metric they all optimize for: Time to First Call (TTFC).

The benchmarks tell the story:
→ Under 2 minutes: 3-4x higher developer adoption
→ Over 10 minutes: 50-70% early-stage quit rate

Stripe's "7 lines of code" philosophy turned docs into their primary sales channel. Postman's ready-to-run collections cut TTFC by 1.7x from a 17-minute baseline.

The biggest gap? The sandbox-to-production transition. Developers who make a successful test call but never ship to production represent lost revenue. That transition (key rotation, webhook config, rate limits) is where guided onboarding has the highest impact.
