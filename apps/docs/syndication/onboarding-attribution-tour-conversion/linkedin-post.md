Your onboarding flow has five tours. A user completes three, skips two, then upgrades to paid. Which tour gets credit?

Most product teams can't answer this. They track "onboarding completion rate" as a single number and move on. But that's like measuring marketing ROI by counting total ad impressions.

We applied multi-touch attribution models (the same ones marketing teams use for channels) inside the product, at the individual tour level. Two findings stood out:

1. U-shaped attribution (40% first touch / 40% last touch / 20% middle) was the most useful starting point for teams with fewer than 500 conversions per month.

2. Running a holdout group (15% of users saw no tours) revealed that 2 of 5 tours had near-zero conversion impact despite strong completion metrics.

95% of SaaS companies misattribute revenue with single-touch models. That's just the marketing side. Inside the product, most teams track zero attribution data for in-app guidance.
