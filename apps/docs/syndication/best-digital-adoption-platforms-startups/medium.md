# The Startup's Guide to Digital Adoption Platforms in 2026

## Most DAP comparisons are written for enterprises. This one isn't.

*Originally published at [usertourkit.com](https://usertourkit.com/blog/best-digital-adoption-platforms-startups)*

The digital adoption platform market hit $1 billion in 2024. WalkMe got acquired by SAP for $1.5 billion. Pendo's paid plans start at $7,000 a year. Gartner says 70% of large enterprises will run a DAP by end of 2025.

None of that helps a 12-person startup trying to reduce churn.

We tested seven platforms by building the same onboarding flow: a welcome tour, a feature checklist, and an NPS survey. Here's what we found, with real pricing numbers.

*Disclosure: We built Tour Kit, one of the tools on this list. Every claim is verifiable against public data.*

## The pricing landscape

Startup-tier DAP pricing ranges from free to $249 per month as of April 2026:

- **HelpHero:** $55/month (cheapest SaaS option, 1,000 MAU)
- **UserGuiding:** $89/month (fastest no-code setup)
- **Hopscotch:** $99/month (most transparent pricing, 3,000 MAU)
- **Tour Kit:** Free core (MIT) + $99 one-time Pro license
- **Shepherd.js:** Free (MIT, open-source)
- **Appcues:** $249/month (best no-code builder)
- **Pendo Free:** $0 for 500 MAU, then ~$7,000/year

## Three paths for startups

**Path 1: Developer library.** If you have React developers who want code-level control, Tour Kit or Shepherd.js give you smaller bundles, design system integration, and no per-MAU pricing. Tour Kit adds checklists, surveys, and analytics. Shepherd.js handles tours only.

**Path 2: Budget SaaS.** If your team needs something running without engineering time, HelpHero ($55/mo), UserGuiding ($89/mo), or Hopscotch ($99/mo) get you there. HelpHero is cheapest. UserGuiding has the most features at entry tier.

**Path 3: Full-stack platform.** If you've raised funding and product managers need to create flows independently, Appcues has the best builder. Pendo Free works if you're under 500 users, but the jump to paid ($7K/year) is steep.

## The one thing to avoid

Don't sign an annual contract with an enterprise DAP (WalkMe, Whatfix) before product-market fit. Enterprise tools assume stable usage and large teams. Startups have neither.
