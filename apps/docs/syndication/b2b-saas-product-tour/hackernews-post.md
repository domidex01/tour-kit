## Title: Product Tours for B2B SaaS: Activation Benchmarks by Category and Role-Based Patterns

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/b2b-saas-product-tour

## Comment to post immediately after:

I compiled activation benchmarks across five B2B SaaS categories (developer tools, project management, CRM, analytics, marketing automation) and found the spread is wider than expected: developer tools activate at 30-45% while CRM tools sit at 15-25%.

The biggest takeaway from the research: role-based segmentation (admin vs end-user vs manager) outperforms generic tours by 20-30%. Each role has a different activation milestone, and serving them all with one tour is where most B2B onboarding breaks.

The article includes code examples in TypeScript/React, a bundle size comparison of tour libraries (from <12KB to 37KB gzipped), and the accessibility requirements that the European Accessibility Act now mandates for SaaS products accessible to EU users.

Full disclosure: I built Tour Kit, the headless library referenced in the article. The benchmarks come from external sources (Product Growth Intelligence, Smashing Magazine, Custify) and are linked throughout.
