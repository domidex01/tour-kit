Most B2B product tours are built to be completed, not to drive activation. There's a meaningful difference.

I compiled activation benchmarks across five B2B SaaS categories and the spread is wider than you'd expect: developer tools activate at 30-45%, while CRM tools sit at 15-25%. The gap between median and top quartile is explained almost entirely by onboarding quality.

The pattern that consistently works: segment tours by role. Admins need SSO setup. End-users need their first workflow. Managers need reporting. One generic tour can't serve all three, and personalizing by role reduces time-to-value by 40%.

Four-step tours hit 60.1% completion. Over five steps? Below 20%. Keep it short, keep it role-specific, and measure activation events instead of step clicks.
