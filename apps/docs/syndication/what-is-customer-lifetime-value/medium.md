# The SaaS Metric Your Codebase Controls More Than Marketing Does

## Customer lifetime value has a churn denominator — and onboarding is the biggest lever on it

*Originally published at [usertourkit.com](https://usertourkit.com/blog/what-is-customer-lifetime-value)*

Customer lifetime value gets talked about in board decks and marketing dashboards. But the variable that moves it most sits in your codebase, in how your product teaches new users what it does.

Here's the formula, why churn dominates it, and what that means for the engineers building onboarding flows.

## The formula

The SaaS subscription formula:

CLV = (ARPU x Gross Margin) / Churn Rate

A worked example: with $20,000 annual revenue per account, 80% gross margin, and 2.5% monthly churn, CLV equals $640,000.

Notice where churn sits. It's in the denominator. Halving your churn rate roughly doubles CLV. Not a linear relationship, an exponential one. That's the math behind every exec who says "retention matters more than acquisition."

## What the numbers look like

At $20K ARPA with 80% margin:

- 5% monthly churn = $320,000 CLV (baseline)
- 2.5% monthly churn = $640,000 CLV (+100%)
- 1.5% monthly churn = $1,066,667 CLV (+233%)

Customer acquisition costs have risen 40-60% since 2023, which means the retention side carries even more weight now.

## Why onboarding is the biggest CLV lever

Harvard Business Review argues that standard CLV calculations miss something important: they don't account for how customers become more valuable over time through capability growth. Good onboarding doesn't just prevent churn. It builds the competence that drives expansion revenue.

The numbers back this up. Poor onboarding causes 23% of SaaS customer churn, and users who don't find value in the first week face 90% churn probability. A 25% increase in activation rates? That correlates with a 34% revenue boost.

Customers who complete onboarding are 21% more likely to adopt the full product and 12% less likely to churn in year one. They're also 30% more likely to purchase additional services.

## Product tours and CLV

Interactive product tours increase feature adoption by 42% compared to static documentation. Click-triggered tours achieve a 67% completion rate while delay-triggered tours hit only 31%. Tours with 3-4 steps reach 72-74% completion, the sweet spot.

But tour completion alone doesn't predict retention. What matters is whether tours drive meaningful activation events, the actions that correlate with long-term usage.

Engineering choices matter here: bundle size, accessibility, and personalization capability all affect whether users actually complete onboarding and activate.
