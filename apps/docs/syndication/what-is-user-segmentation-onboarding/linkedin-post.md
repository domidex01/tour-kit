Your admin user and your free-trial signup don't need the same product tour.

Yet most SaaS apps show identical onboarding to everyone. The result: power users get bored, new users get overwhelmed, and activation suffers.

User segmentation fixes this. Group users by role, behavior, experience level, or plan tier — then show each group a different tour. The numbers are compelling: SocialPilot reported 20% higher activation and 15% less churn. Personalized paths boost completion rates by roughly 35%.

There are six segmentation types that matter for product tours. I wrote a breakdown with a comparison table and a React code pattern for implementing segment-based tour targeting in under 20 lines.

The angle that surprised me: W3C accessibility guidelines explicitly recommend personalization for cognitive and motor needs. Segmented onboarding isn't just better UX — it's where compliance standards are heading.
