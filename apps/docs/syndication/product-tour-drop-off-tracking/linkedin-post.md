Most product tours track completion rate. That's it. One number.

But completion rate can't tell you WHY users leave. Is it step 2 (bad targeting)? Step 4 (confusing content)? The "invite your team" step that nobody wants to do before they've seen the product?

Chameleon analyzed 550 million tour interactions and found a pattern that should concern every product team: 4-step tours complete at 74%, but 5-step tours drop to 34%.

One extra step. Half your users gone.

Step-level drop-off tracking fixes this. Instead of "did they finish?" you get "exactly which step lost them, how long they hesitated before leaving, and whether they actively skipped or passively abandoned."

I wrote up the full implementation — event instrumentation, funnel visualization in PostHog and Mixpanel, time-on-step analysis, and benchmark ranges so you know what "good" looks like.
