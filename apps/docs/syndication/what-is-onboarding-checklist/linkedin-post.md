The average onboarding checklist completion rate is 19.2%. The median is 10.1%.

That's across 188 SaaS companies. The data from Userpilot's 2025 benchmark tells an interesting story about scale.

Startups ($1-5M revenue) see 27.1% completion. Mid-market ($10-50M) drops to 15%. The reason? Smaller teams keep checklists to 3-5 focused tasks. Larger teams let feature creep turn checklists into miniature product roadmaps.

Miller's Law gives us the ceiling: 5-7 items in working memory. Go past that and completion craters regardless of how polished your UI is.

I wrote a developer-focused breakdown covering the data, three design principles that actually move the number, and an accessible React implementation using headless component architecture.

The accessibility angle was the biggest gap I found — none of the existing content on onboarding checklists covers WCAG progressbar roles, keyboard navigation, or screen reader announcements.
