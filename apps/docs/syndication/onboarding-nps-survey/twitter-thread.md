**1/** Most teams ship an NPS survey after onboarding and never look at it again.

The score sits in a dashboard. Detractors churn. Promoters never get asked for a referral.

Here's how to actually use onboarding NPS: 🧵

**2/** Timing matters more than the question.

Ask immediately after the last tour step? Feels aggressive.
Wait 7+ days? You're measuring general product sentiment, not onboarding quality.

The sweet spot: 24-72 hours after onboarding completion.

**3/** The calculation is simple (% Promoters - % Detractors), but sample size kills you.

NPS with fewer than 30 responses has a ±15 point margin of error.

We were making product decisions on 12 responses. Don't do that.

**4/** Benchmarks for context:

- B2B SaaS self-serve: 20-40
- Enterprise with guided onboarding: 30-55
- Developer tools: 15-30 (devs are harsher critics)

Above 50 = excellent
Below 10 = broken onboarding
Negative = stop acquiring, start fixing

**5/** The biggest win: close the loop with detractors within 48 hours.

Not solving the problem — just acknowledging it.

"Hey, you mentioned the API setup was confusing. We're rewriting that section this sprint."

Detractors who hear back are 2.3x more likely to stay (Retently data).
