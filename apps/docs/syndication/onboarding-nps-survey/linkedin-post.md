Most SaaS teams collect onboarding NPS and do nothing with it.

The score sits in a dashboard. Detractors churn silently. Promoters never get asked for a referral. And the product team wonders why activation rates aren't improving.

After working through the data, three things stood out:

The 24-72 hour window after onboarding completion is the sweet spot for triggering NPS. Ask immediately and it feels aggressive. Wait past a week and you're measuring general product sentiment, not onboarding quality.

Sample size under 30 makes NPS meaningless. The margin of error exceeds ±15 points. Don't make product decisions on 12 responses.

Closing the loop with detractors within 48 hours has more impact than any survey optimization. Detractors who hear back from a human are 2.3x more likely to stay (Retently data).

B2B SaaS benchmarks for context: self-serve products score 20-40, enterprise with guided onboarding scores 30-55, developer tools land 15-30.
