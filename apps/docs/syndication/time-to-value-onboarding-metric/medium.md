# Time to Value: The Onboarding Metric That Actually Predicts Revenue

*Why completion rate is lying to you, and what to track instead*

*Originally published at [usertourkit.com](https://usertourkit.com/blog/time-to-value-onboarding-metric)*

Your onboarding completion rate looks great. 78% of new users finish the tour. But half of them churn within a month.

The disconnect? Completion measures whether users clicked through your steps. It says nothing about whether they reached the moment your product became worth keeping. That moment has a name: time to value.

Teams that measured TTV instead of completion rate saw 15-25% higher trial-to-paid conversion, according to Intercom benchmarks reported by Userpilot.

## What is time to value?

Time to value (TTV) measures the elapsed time between a user's first interaction with your product and the moment they experience its core benefit. As of April 2026, the average TTV across 547 SaaS companies is 1 day, 12 hours, and 23 minutes.

The formula: subtract the signup timestamp from the moment the user experiences value.

"Value experienced" is product-specific. For a project management tool, it might be the first task completed with a teammate. For a developer tool, it's often the first successful API call.

## Why this matters more than you think

More than 98% of users who don't experience product value within two weeks will churn. That number comes from Amplitude's analysis of activation curves across thousands of products. The decline is steep: 21% activation on day 1 drops to 12% by day 7 and 9% by day 14.

And 69% of products with strong early activation also showed strong three-month retention. Speed to value predicts revenue.

## The benchmarks

Userpilot's benchmark report (n=547 SaaS companies) provides the most comprehensive dataset:

- Average TTV: 1 day, 12 hours, 23 minutes
- Median TTV: 1 day, 1 hour, 54 minutes
- Top quartile (2026): Under 5 minutes
- High churn threshold: Over 7 days

The gap between average and top quartile is striking. Five minutes versus a day and a half.

One counterintuitive finding: PLG and sales-led companies have nearly identical TTV (1 day 12 hours vs 1 day 11 hours). The assumption that self-serve is always faster doesn't hold.

## Five ways to reduce TTV

**1. Cut guided flows to five steps or fewer.** Chameleon's dataset of 15 million interactions shows 3-step tours have 72% completion. At 7+ steps, completion falls to 16%.

**2. Trigger guides on user intent, not on page load.** Nearly 70% of users skip auto-triggered flows. User-initiated guides outperform by 2-3x.

**3. Segment by role.** Canva saw a 10% activation increase with role-specific onboarding.

**4. Use empty states as starting points.** The moment a user sees an empty dashboard is the highest-churn moment and the best moment to guide them.

**5. Measure against activation, not in isolation.** If tour completion doesn't correlate with activation, the tour isn't helping.

## The revenue math

Companies that reduced onboarding time by 30% recognized revenue an average of 3 months sooner (onramp.us, 2026 State of Onboarding).

Real results: Correcto went from 17.4% to 53.5% activation. Senja grew MRR from $0 to $33K. Rocketbots increased MRR 300%.
