## Title: Time to Value: SaaS benchmarks from 547 companies show top quartile hits under 5 minutes

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/time-to-value-onboarding-metric

## Comment to post immediately after:

I've been digging into time to value (TTV) as a replacement for onboarding completion rate. The Userpilot benchmark report (n=547 SaaS companies) has some interesting data:

Average TTV is 1 day 12 hours. Top quartile products get users to value in under 5 minutes. Products exceeding 7 days face dramatically higher churn.

The most counterintuitive finding: PLG and sales-led companies have nearly identical TTV (1d 12h vs 1d 11h). The "self-serve is faster" assumption doesn't hold — what matters is how well you guide users to their first valuable action, regardless of whether a human is involved.

Amplitude's retention data makes the case for measuring this: 98% of users who don't experience value within two weeks churn. And 69% of products with strong early activation also had strong three-month retention. Speed to value is the strongest leading indicator of revenue retention I've found.

The article includes TTV calculation formulas, industry benchmarks by vertical (CRM averages 1d 5h, HR tools average 3d 19h), and five specific patterns for reducing TTV through product tours. Also includes React code examples for instrumenting TTV tracking.

Honest limitation: these benchmarks come primarily from Userpilot and Chameleon (both onboarding tool vendors), so take the exact numbers with appropriate context. The directional insights are solid though.
