Switching your onboarding tool costs 150-200% of your annual contract value.

But the money isn't the real problem. The real cost is content migration.

When your product team has built 40+ tours, checklists, and surveys in a proprietary visual builder over two years, switching means recreating every single one from scratch. There's no export-import path between the major platforms.

Zluri calls this "process lock-in" — your team's workflows and institutional knowledge are tied to one vendor's interface. It's invisible at contract time and devastating at renewal time.

Some data points from research:
- 62% of organizations use open source specifically to avoid vendor lock-in (Percona)
- The average enterprise manages 291 SaaS apps in 2026, up from 110 in 2020
- The EU Data Act (effective Sept 2025) now mandates machine-readable export formats

I wrote up a breakdown of four types of vendor lock-in applied specifically to onboarding tools, with examples from Pendo, Appcues, and Userpilot: https://usertourkit.com/blog/what-is-vendor-lock-in
