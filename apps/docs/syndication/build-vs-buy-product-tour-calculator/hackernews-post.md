## Title: Three-year TCO for product tour approaches: DIY ($142K), library ($48K), SaaS ($36K-$99K)

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/build-vs-buy-product-tour-calculator

## Comment to post immediately after:

I spent a week gathering cost data for three approaches to implementing product tours: building from scratch, using a headless library, and paying for SaaS platforms (Appcues, Pendo, Userpilot).

The most interesting finding was the MAU crossover point. Below ~25K MAUs, SaaS is genuinely cheaper. Above that, per-user pricing compounds and the library path wins on raw cost. At 100K MAUs, SaaS platforms bill $100K+ over three years for code you don't own.

I included the actual TCO formulas so anyone can plug in their own numbers. The variables that matter most: developer hourly rate and MAU count. The Atlassian case study ($3M over three years for internal onboarding) came from a Userpilot analysis and was more dramatic than I expected.

Disclosure: I built Tour Kit, a headless tour library, so my bias toward the library path is obvious. I tried to steelman both DIY and SaaS throughout — there are clear situations where each wins.

Maintenance data came from IBM research (50-75% of lifetime software costs). Developer rate ranges from HatchWorks and Bureau of Labor Statistics. SaaS pricing from Appcues/Pendo as of April 2026.
