**1/** We calculated 3-year TCO for three product tour approaches: DIY, open-source library, and SaaS.

The results change depending on one variable most teams never consider.

Thread with actual numbers:

**2/** Year 1 costs:
- DIY from scratch: $70K-$85K
- Headless library: $18K-$36K
- SaaS (Appcues/Pendo): $6K-$54K

SaaS wins year one for small teams. But year one is the misleading part.

**3/** Three-year TCO tells a different story:
- DIY: $142,500
- Library: $48,000
- SaaS at 10K MAUs: $36,000
- SaaS at 50K MAUs: $99,000+

The crossover: ~25K MAUs. Below that, buy SaaS. Above that, libraries win.

**4/** The number that surprised us most:

IBM research shows maintenance consumes 50-75% of total software costs.

Atlassian spent $3M over three years building onboarding internally. Started with 3 people, ended with 7. Infrastructure alone was $200K-$500K/year.

**5/** The thing nobody talks about: "headless library" as a third category.

Every build-vs-buy article is binary. But a headless library gives you:
- Zero vendor lock-in
- Full design control
- No per-MAU pricing
- 90% less code than full DIY
