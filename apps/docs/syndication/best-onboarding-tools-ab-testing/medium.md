# 7 Onboarding Tools with A/B Testing: What Actually Works in 2026

## Most onboarding platforms claim A/B testing. We tested what that actually means.

Around 70% of new customer acquisitions fail within the first two months of onboarding. A/B testing your onboarding flows should help, but the gap between "we have A/B testing" on a pricing page and "you can run meaningful experiments" is wider than you'd expect.

We tested seven tools across six criteria: variant depth, developer experience, accessibility, performance, pricing transparency, and analytics integration.

Full disclosure: Tour Kit is our project. Every data point is verifiable against npm, GitHub, or vendor docs.

## The quick version

**Tour Kit** (free, MIT): Headless React library. Unlimited variants, WCAG 2.1 AA compliant, under 8KB gzipped. Requires developers.

**Appcues** (enterprise pricing): No-code flow variation testing with flexible splits. StoryboardThat saw 112% conversion increase. Control groups still "coming soon."

**Userpilot** (growth tier): Three A/B test types plus 14 survey templates. Winner auto-scaling. Pricing not transparent.

**Pendo** (Guides Pro): Two variants max, 95% confidence threshold, deep analytics integration.

**Chameleon** (Growth+): AI Copilot auto-generates test variants from existing tours. Unique approach, pricing opaque.

**Statsig** (freemium): Developer-first experimentation platform. Not an onboarding tool; you build the UI.

**Firebase A/B Testing** (free): New for web as of March 2026. Zero cost in Google's ecosystem, adds 35KB.

## The accessibility gap

The biggest finding: not a single onboarding tool verifies that A/B tested variants meet WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility standards. If your product serves users with disabilities, you're on your own for ensuring both experiment variants are accessible.

## How to choose

The $840 million A/B testing tools market splits on one axis: who runs the experiments?

**Engineering team** owns onboarding? Go code-first. Tour Kit for accessible UI; Statsig for raw experimentation.

**Product team** needs to ship without engineering? Go no-code. Appcues, Userpilot, or Chameleon. Budget $300-500+/month.

**Already in an ecosystem?** Pendo for analytics teams. Firebase if you're on Google's stack.
