## Title: Why onboarding SaaS tools charge per MAU (and what it costs at scale)

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/mau-pricing-onboarding-tool

## Comment to post immediately after:

I pulled together pricing data from every major onboarding SaaS vendor (Appcues, Pendo, Userpilot, Chameleon, UserGuiding) to understand how per-MAU billing actually scales. The data came from vendor pricing pages and Vendr's marketplace (530 real Pendo transactions, for example).

The finding that surprised me most: Pendo's median enterprise contract is $48,500/year, with some deals reaching $147,264/year. At those numbers, onboarding tooling becomes a line item that scales with your success rather than your usage.

I build an open-source product tour library, so I have obvious skin in the game here. But the pricing data is sourced and verifiable. The structural argument I'm making is that per-MAU pricing misaligns incentives: the vendor profits when users log in, regardless of whether those users generate revenue for the customer.

The counterpoint (which I tried to treat fairly in the article): infrastructure costs genuinely scale with MAU, the model is simple, and at $10M ARR a $50K/year tool is 0.5% of revenue. The model works for stable-growth products. It breaks for freemium, seasonal, or viral products.
