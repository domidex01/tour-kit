The average mid-size company manages 130+ SaaS subscriptions. That number keeps climbing even as 62% of businesses actively try to consolidate.

I ran the 3-year cost math on onboarding tools specifically, and the numbers tell a clear story:

Entry-tier subscriptions range from $55-249/month. Over 36 months, that's $1,980 to $8,964+ — for functionality that disappears the day you cancel.

For bootstrapped teams, there's a cost structure asymmetry most vendor pricing pages don't surface. Hosted platforms (Appcues, Pendo) maintain servers, CDNs, and analytics databases. That infrastructure justifies recurring revenue. But client-side libraries that ship as a bundle in your app have near-zero marginal cost. When those tools charge monthly, you're paying for the business model, not the infrastructure.

78% of finance decision-makers now report increased scrutiny of recurring software costs. 41% express interest in one-time purchase alternatives.

The subscription model isn't irrational. But for a bootstrapped team that won't optimize for ARR multiples or plan an exit, perpetual licensing often makes more financial sense.
