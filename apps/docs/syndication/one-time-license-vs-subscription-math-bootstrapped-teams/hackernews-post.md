## Title: The 3-year cost of onboarding tool subscriptions for bootstrapped teams

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/one-time-license-vs-subscription-math-bootstrapped-teams

## Comment to post immediately after:

I spent time calculating what bootstrapped teams actually pay for onboarding/product tour tools over 3 years. Entry-level subscriptions range from $55-249/month, which adds up to $1,980-$8,964+ over 36 months.

The part I found most interesting was the cost structure asymmetry. Hosted platforms (Appcues, Pendo) have genuine ongoing infrastructure costs that justify subscriptions — servers, CDNs, analytics databases. But client-side JavaScript libraries that ship as a bundle in your app have near-zero marginal cost per customer. When those tools charge monthly, you're paying for the business model, not the infrastructure.

Some data points from the research: 78% of finance decision-makers report increased scrutiny of recurring costs (Getmonetizely 2026), 62% are consolidating SaaS portfolios, and roughly 40% of AppSumo lifetime deal products shut down within 3 years.

I build Tour Kit (open-source headless tour library for React), so I have a clear bias toward the one-time license model. The article acknowledges that — and also covers when subscriptions genuinely make more sense (visual builders, server-side targeting, teams without developers).

Happy to discuss the economics or methodology. All pricing data is from vendor websites as of April 2026.
