# What Is Product-Led Growth? And Why Most Users Never Activate

## How product tours close the gap between signup and "aha moment"

*Originally published at [usertourkit.com](https://usertourkit.com/blog/what-is-product-led-growth)*

Product-led growth is the reason you can sign up for Figma, Notion, or Slack and start using them before talking to anyone in sales. The product sells itself. No demo calls, no "request access" forms. You try it, you see the value, you stay.

As of April 2026, 58% of companies use a PLG model according to Mixpanel's State of Digital Analytics report covering 12,000+ companies. But PLG only works if new users reach their "aha moment" fast enough.

---

## The definition

Product-led growth (PLG) is a go-to-market strategy where the product itself drives customer acquisition, activation, conversion, and expansion. Instead of relying on sales teams or marketing funnels, PLG companies build self-serve experiences that let users extract value before paying. Blake Bartlett at OpenView Venture Partners coined the term in 2016 to describe what Dropbox and Slack were already doing.

PLG isn't a feature. It's an organizational model. Every team orients around the product experience rather than sales processes.

## How the loop works

PLG follows a self-reinforcing loop: users sign up for free, experience core value quickly, convert to paid plans, and invite colleagues who repeat the cycle.

Three mechanics make the loop spin:

**Low-friction entry.** Dropbox grew to 700 million users and $2.3 billion in annual revenue through freemium and referrals.

**Fast time-to-value.** Top PLG products get users to their first "wow moment" in under five minutes.

**Built-in virality.** Calendly hit 20 million users and a $3 billion valuation because every meeting link is a product demo.

PLG businesses grow 20-30% faster on average and are valued 30% higher than the public SaaS Index Fund average.

## The activation problem

Here's what PLG articles don't tell you: signing up is the easy part. Average activation rates sit around 40% for single-user products and drop to 20% for multi-user tools. The majority of signups never reach the moment where they understand why your product exists.

Product tours fix this. AB Tasty redesigned their onboarding with guided tours and saw tour completion jump from 2% to 15%. Free trial-to-PQL conversion went from 0% to 20%.

A 10% improvement in activation rate typically produces a bigger revenue impact than a 10% increase in signups.

68% of developers who abandon trials cite "too much setup time" as the reason. Only 12% cite pricing. Product tours attack the real problem.

## What this means for React teams

If you're building a PLG product in React, your onboarding experience is your growth engine. Tools like Appcues and Pendo inject scripts and charge per MAU. Open-source alternatives like Tour Kit give you code-level control over activation flows without vendor lock-in.
