Your product tour has a 61% completion rate. Great number. But does it actually predict retention?

We analyzed benchmark data from 15 million product tour interactions and found that completion rate alone is misleading. Two users "complete" the same tour — one engages deeply, the other clicks through in 8 seconds. Both count equally.

The biggest surprise: trigger type creates a 2x completion gap. Click-triggered tours complete at 67%. Auto-popup tours complete at 31%. And 4-step tours hit 74% completion, while 5-step tours drop to 34%.

But the real insight comes from behavioral cohort analysis — splitting users by what they did *after* the tour, not just whether they finished. Slack discovered this when they found that teams sending 2,000+ messages retained dramatically better, and redesigned onboarding around that behavior. 30-day retention jumped 17%.
