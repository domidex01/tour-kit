The no-code/low-code market hit $34.7B in 2025. By 2026, 70% of new enterprise applications will use these tools.

But here's the onboarding problem nobody is solving: the average checklist completion rate across SaaS products is just 19.2% (Userpilot, n=188 companies). No-code platforms have it worse because they're onboarding non-technical users to genuinely complex visual editors.

After researching the data, three patterns consistently improve activation:

→ Dependency-based checklists instead of linear tours (cap at 7 items)
→ Contextual hints triggered by user state, not page load
→ Persona segmentation — the admin who signs up isn't always the person who builds

The biggest gap I found: accessibility compliance. With 80% of low-code users being business technologists by 2026, you have hundreds of untrained citizen developers building apps. The European Accessibility Act (June 2025) and record ADA lawsuits (2024) make this a legal risk, not just a UX one.

Teams iterating on onboarding weekly see 25% higher activation. A 25% activation lift correlates with 34% MRR growth.
