## Title: The $3.6B onboarding tax – how SaaS tools extract developer value

## URL: https://usertourkit.com/blog/onboarding-saas-tax

## Comment to post immediately after:

I spent time sizing the onboarding/digital adoption platform market and tracing where the money actually flows.

Key findings: the combined DAP + onboarding software market is ~$3.6B in 2026 (sources: Fortune Business Insights, Market Research Future, Valuates Reports). SAP's $1.5B acquisition of WalkMe in 2024 was the clearest signal of how valuable vendor lock-in is in this space.

The structural issue I found interesting: in every market segment, the buyer (product manager) is different from the maintainer (developer). This creates a misalignment where vendor incentives favor PM demos over developer experience. The subscription fee ends up being the smallest component of total cost — 65% of software costs happen post-deployment.

Performance data from SpeedCurve: pages with third-party scripts enabled can hit 26.82s LCP vs <1s without. The average site loads ~20 external scripts.

Disclosure: I built Tour Kit, an open-source onboarding library for React. The article uses data from independent market research firms, not my own metrics.

Limitations I acknowledge in the article: building from scratch costs $60K-$3.5M depending on scope, and no-code builders genuinely solve a real problem for non-technical product teams.
