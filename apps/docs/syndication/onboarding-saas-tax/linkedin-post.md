SAP paid $1.5 billion for WalkMe. Not for the technology. For access to the enterprises already locked in.

The combined onboarding and digital adoption market is now $3.6 billion. Here's what nobody talks about: the people who pay for these tools aren't the people who maintain them.

Product managers buy Pendo ($48K/year avg), Appcues ($300/mo), or Userpilot ($299/mo). Engineering teams absorb the integration cost — the CSS conflicts, the bundle bloat, the quarterly vendor updates, the annual migrations.

65% of total software costs happen after initial deployment. The subscription fee is the smallest part of the bill.

I built an open-source alternative (User Tour Kit) because I believe onboarding logic is 200 lines of TypeScript, not a 45KB vendor script your team can't tree-shake.
