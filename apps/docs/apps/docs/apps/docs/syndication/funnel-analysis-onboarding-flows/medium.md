# Funnel Analysis for Onboarding Flows: What Most Teams Get Wrong

*Originally published at [usertourkit.com](https://usertourkit.com/blog/funnel-analysis-onboarding-flows)*

Most onboarding funnels measure the wrong thing. They track whether users finished the flow and call it a day. But completion rate and activation quality are different metrics, and conflating them hides the exact problems that cause churn.

A user who clicks "Next" through every tooltip has a 100% completion rate. A user who skips the tour, opens the dashboard, creates their first project, and invites a teammate has a 0% completion rate. Which one churns?

The second user activated. The first one didn't.

As of April 2026, average SaaS activation rates sit at 37.5%. Companies that increase activation by 25% see a corresponding 34% revenue bump.

## The Five Stages

Every onboarding funnel follows the same skeleton: signup, activation, habit formation, retention, and expansion.

**Signup to activation** is where 79% of funnel drop-off happens. Products that get time-to-value under 5 minutes convert free trials above 25%.

**Activation to habit** requires Day-7 retention above 40%. Below 30% means users activated but didn't stick.

**Retention to expansion** covers upsells and feature upgrades. Proactive onboarding drives 20-40% increases in expansion revenue.

## The Metric That Gets Ignored

Analytics tools cannot determine whether a user is navigating with a screen reader. Google Analytics has no plans to add this. Your funnel data is structurally incomplete for users on assistive technology.

## Industry Benchmarks

A FinTech app with 5% activation and an AI tool with 54.8% activation are both normal for their categories.

- AI/ML tools: 54.8% activation rate
- B2B SaaS average: 37.5%
- FinTech: 5%
- Freemium: 25-35%

## The Improvement Loop

1. Find the biggest leak (highest drop-off step)
2. Watch session replays at that point to understand why
3. Fix it and measure activation quality, not just completion
4. Repeat with the next biggest leak
