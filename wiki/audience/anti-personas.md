*Who NOT to target. Saying "this isn't for you" is a positioning feature, not a bug.*

| Anti-persona | Why not | What to say / redirect to |
|---|---|---|
| **Enterprise needing no-code builders** (500+ employees) | Code-first by design, no visual editor | "Try WalkMe or Pendo — purpose-built for that workflow." |
| **Teams without React** (Vue, Angular, Svelte) | Architecture is fundamentally React-native | "Try Shepherd.js or Driver.js." |
| **Desktop app overlays** (Electron) | Web DOM only | "You need a native overlay solution." |
| **SOC 2 / HIPAA compliance buyers** | No server component to audit | "No data leaves the browser (privacy advantage), but we can't provide vendor certifications." |
| **Non-technical marketers without eng support** | Requires writing React code | "Try Appcues or UserGuiding — they have visual builders." |

## Why this matters

Being explicit about who TourKit is NOT for does three things:

1. Filters low-quality leads before they hit support
2. Signals authenticity to the Frontend Lead ICP ("they know what they're not")
3. Earns goodwill in communities by redirecting to the right tool instead of pretending to fit

## How to apply

- Docs homepage has a "Not the right tool if..." section
- HN / Reddit comments explicitly mention tradeoffs
- Sales conversations lead with qualification questions
- Comparison pages don't claim universal superiority
