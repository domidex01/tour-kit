*Solo Founder / Indie Hacker. 30% of marketing effort.*

## Who

Building a SaaS solo or with 1–2 co-founders. Full-stack, chose React/Next.js. Revenue $0–$30K MRR. Bootstrapped or lightly funded. Age 24–40.

## Stack (the "2026 indie stack")

Next.js 14/15 · TypeScript · Tailwind + shadcn/ui · Prisma/Drizzle · Vercel · Stripe · Better Auth / Clerk.

## Top pain points

1. SaaS tour tools eat slim margins — $300/mo = 6% of revenue at $5K MRR
2. Subscription fatigue — already paying for hosting, auth, email, analytics...
3. Needs something that works in an afternoon
4. Wants native shadcn/ui look out of the box
5. Building in public means the stack gets visibility; quality matters

## Buying triggers

1. Product Hunt launch feedback says onboarding is confusing
2. Hit $1K MRR, optimizing activation
3. Saw a peer tweet about TourKit
4. Evaluating SaaS stack for one-time vs recurring costs

## Decision process

**0–15 minutes from discovery to working tour.** The entire funnel is measured in minutes. Getting-started experience IS the sales pitch.

## Key message

> "Product tours for your Next.js SaaS. Free tier forever. Pro is $99 once — not $300/month."

## Channels

Indie hacker Twitter (@levelsio, @marc_louvion, @shpigford) · Product Hunt · r/SaaS · r/indiehackers · YouTube (Marc Lou, Simon Hoiberg, Fireship).

## What resonates

- Price in the headline — "$99 once"
- "Works in an afternoon"
- Code block they can copy-paste
- shadcn-native look (matches what they already have)
- Free tier with no gotchas
- "Ship it today" energy

## Sales objections relevant to this ICP

See [audience/objections.md](objections.md). Most relevant:

- "$99 seems too cheap"
- "Don't trust paid open-source"
- "We should build our own"
