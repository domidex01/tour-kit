## 6 content pillars

1. **Tutorials & How-Tos** — Capture problem-aware devs (`/blog/how-to-[action]-[tech]`)
2. **Comparison & Migration** — Highest conversion, high-intent buyers (`/compare/tour-kit-vs-[x]`)
3. **Technical Deep-Dives** — Authority building with senior devs
4. **Use Case Showcases** — Help users envision TourKit for their problem
5. **Ecosystem & Integration** — Capture "[tool A] + [tool B]" stack searches
6. **Product Updates** — Signal active maintenance, feed newsletters

## Why SEO is the moat

- SaaS competitors (UserGuiding, Chameleon, Whatfix, Userpilot) dominate every product tour keyword with blog content
- **No OSS library has a meaningful content presence**
- Every comparison page drives traffic for years — compounding asset
- Documentation itself ranks — see [brand/positioning.md](../brand/positioning.md) "Documentation IS marketing"

## Content generation guardrails

When writing content:

1. Start from the wiki — `brand/voice.md`, `audience/*`, `competitors/*`
2. Follow the tone rules — no banned words, no emojis in blog
3. Every claim backed by code, numbers, or a link
4. For comparison pages: factual, fair, acknowledge where the competitor is stronger
5. File new insights back into the wiki — compare pages often surface new data points
